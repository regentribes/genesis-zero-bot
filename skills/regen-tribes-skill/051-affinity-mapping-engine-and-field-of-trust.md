Trust emerges from repeated interaction.
Trust follows a formula.
Trust equals evidence divided by claims.
Evidence is what you do.
Claims are what you say.
High evidence over claims means high trust.
Low evidence over claims means low trust.
High claims over evidence means reputation inflation.

Trust is not binary. It is a ratio. The ratio shifts with every action. Every interaction updates the ratio. Trust is dynamic. Trust is measurable. Trust is comparable.

A group with high FOT coordinates without hierarchy. Coordination emerges from mutual evidence. People adjust based on observed behavior. No central authority needed. No enforcement needed.

A group with low FOT needs rules and enforcement.

Enforcement compensates for lack of evidence.
The group expends energy on control.
Energy not available for production.

The goal is to raise the FOT. Raise it by producing evidence. Consistent action over time. Keeping promises. Reduce it by overclaiming. Claims without action erode trust.

The FOT composite is the minimum of five indicators. This is the hologram principle. One weak indicator drags the whole field down. All five must be maintained.

Indicator 1 is reliability. Do you do what you say. Do you show up. Do you deliver.

Indicator 2 is competence. Can you do what you claim. Do you have the skill. The knowledge.

Indicator 3 is integrity. Do you say what you mean. Do you mean what you say.

Indicator 4 is consistency. Do you behave the same over time. Across contexts. Can others predict your behavior.

Indicator 5 is openness. Do you share information. Mistakes. Learning.
