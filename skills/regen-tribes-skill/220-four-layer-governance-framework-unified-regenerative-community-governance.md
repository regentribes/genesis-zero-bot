# Four Layer Governance Framework Unified Regenerative Community Governance

Four frameworks address different layers of the same question: how do humans coordinate at scale under conditions of uncertainty?

Organ (metaphor for a functional subsystem or team):
Layer 1 is Field. Layer 2 is Mechanic. Layer 3 is Organizational. Layer 4 is Epistemic.

Together they form a complete governance loop from sensing collective reality to binding commitments.

Every concept is described by its first principles. No proprietary labels.

Layer 1 is the Field Layer. The AME and Mythogen frameworks answer: what is the state of collective reality?

Layer 2 is the Mechanic Layer. The Chase Hughes framework answers: why do people shift and how is change triggered?

Layer 3 is the Organizational Layer. The AISYSTANT framework answers: who does what by which method producing which artifact?

Layer 4 is the Epistemic Layer. The FPF framework answers: how do we say things precisely so agreements are binding?

The Field of Trust is an emergent plasma state of collective alignment or fragmentation.

It is not an average of individual opinions. It is the minimum of five indicators.

The five indicators are shared narrative, mutual recognition, shared fate, warm data, and regenerative capacity.

Shared narrative: the collective story about past and future.
Mutual recognition: members see and are seen by others.
Shared fate: we rise or fall together.
Warm data: qualitative information about relational quality.
Regenerative capacity: the ability to renew after disruption.

The composite FOT equals the minimum of all five. Raising the minimum indicator raises the composite.

The triune brain framework explains individual behavior change.

The reptilian brain handles survival, territory, and hierarchy. Target with movement and appearance authority signals.

The paleomammalian brain handles emotion, bonding, and grief. Target with connection and ritual signals.

The neomammalian brain handles rationalization and language. Target with confidence and evidence signals.

The mammalian brain responds to imagery, emotion, and repetition. It does not respond to pure logic or language.

The FEAR model: Focus on the role identification, Emotional engagement of subject of interest, Agitation through grief circuits, Repetition through method practice.

The AISYSTANT core triad is role, method, and work artifact.

Role is a functional position. Method is a practice combining discipline and tools. Work artifact is the physical output.

System (metaphor for an integrated whole or network):

The 3x3 table maps supersystem, system of interest, and creation systems against target audience, usage concept, architecture, and creation methods.

Stages are the logical time sequence of methods. Phases are the physical time sequence of tasks with deadlines.

An increment is a small meaningful improvement. An iteration is a hypothesis-test cycle that produces increments.

FPF provides the precision language that makes commitments binding.

U.Signature is a law-governed declaration on a bounded context. It creates a formal commitment.

U.Commitment is a deontic statement that creates binding obligation.

U.EvidenceRole is the accountability role that witnesses and records commitments.

The evidence graph tracks all commitments, evidence of fulfillment, and relationships between them.

The precision upgrade workflow converts fuzzy language to precise form: detect umbrella words, unpack local ontology, choose mathematical substrate, refactor, mint precise lexemes.

The loop begins at field layer sensing.

FOT sensing detects that community coherence is dropping. Specific indicators show the minimum.

This signals the need for behavioral intervention.

The mechanic layer provides the why and how: authority signals must be re-established, limbic brain must be engaged with ritual and emotion.

The organizational layer structures who does what: specific roles perform specific methods using specific artifacts.

The epistemic layer makes commitments binding: formal signatures are recorded in the evidence graph.

The loop returns to field layer sensing to measure whether coherence improved.

Old paradigm words to avoid: shared purpose, consensus, governance as control, leadership as hero.

New paradigm words to use: alignment and combined effect, emergent coherence, field stewardship, role orchestration.

Reason: shared purpose implies fixed endpoint. Alignment is dynamic.

Consensus produces average. Coherence produces minimum of all indicators.

Governance implies hierarchy. Stewardship implies care for wholeness.

Leadership as hero creates fragility. Role rotation creates resilience.

A community is regenerative when all four layers function together.

At field level: FOT composite is above threshold across all five indicators.

At mechanic level: authority signals are distributed, mammalian brain is engaged with ritual and story, grief circuits are honored.

At organizational level: every member plays identified roles, every role uses documented methods, every method produces measurable artifacts, the 3x3 table covers all areas.

At epistemic level: all commitments are in signature form with evidence graph records, fuzzy language is precision-upgraded before adoption, boundary violations route to restoration.

This framework provides the complete governance system for regenerative communities.

Community sensing uses FOT readings. Community behavior uses authority signals and ritual practice. Community organization uses the 3x3 table and role-method-artifact decomposition. Community commitments use FPF signatures and evidence graphs.

The unified loop means the community never operates in only one layer. Sensing without action is paralysis. Action without binding is chaos.
