# The Organ Physical Domain

Organ (metaphor for a functional subsystem or team):

An organ is one neighborhood.
Twelve to 200 people sharing infrastructure.

The standard stack has five parts.
Water. Food. Energy. Waste. Shelter.

Each part closes a loop.
Output of one becomes input of another.
Nothing leaves. Everything cycles.

Water closes its loop this way.
Rain falls on the roof. It flows to the tank.
It is filtered. It is used.
Greywater from sinks goes to the garden.
The constructed wetland cleans the water.
Plants take the nutrients. Clean water returns.

Food closes its loop this way.
A food forest grows on the perimeter.
Aquaponics grows near the center.
Compost turns waste into soil.
Soil grows food. The loop closes.

A food forest has seven layers.
Canopy. Sub-canopy. Shrub. Herb. Ground cover. Root. Vine.

Aquaponics grows fish and plants together.
Fish waste feeds plants. Plants clean the water.
The fish provide protein. The plants provide vegetables.

Energy closes its loop this way.
Solar PV panels charge batteries during the day.
Batteries power the neighborhood at night.
Mesh routing sends power where it is needed.
No central grid required.
The neighborhood disconnects from the external grid.
This is the goal. Energy independence.

Waste closes its loop this way.
All organic waste goes to compost.
Worms process some of it. The rest becomes soil.
Greywater recycles. Blackwater processes separately.

Shelter closes its loop this way.
3D printed construction uses local soil.
No transport cost. No shipping emissions.
Passive design means the building heats and cools itself.
System (metaphor for an integrated whole or network):

No mechanical system needed.

Capitalism prices this at what a household earns in three to six months of labor.
A community builds it for what three households produce together in one season.
Construction time is one week per home.

The standard stack is not mandatory.
Each neighborhood adapts it.
What matters is the loop principle.
Every output becomes an input somewhere else.

A neighborhood that closes its loops is regenerative.
It gives more than it takes.

The organ physical layer is maintained by the organ social layer.
Decisions about infrastructure are made by the organ social layer.
