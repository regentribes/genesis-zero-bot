# Collapse Scenarios Community Resilience Planning

Collapse is not a single event. It is a spectrum of possibilities.

Different futures require different preparations.

**Scenario A: Controlled Transition. Probability 10%.**

Deglobalization happens gradually. Supply chains shrink. Local production grows.

Communities with local food and energy survive and thrive. Others struggle.

The window for preparation is long. The transition is manageable.

Preparation: build local food systems. Build local energy. Build local skills networks.

**Scenario B: Disorderly Collapse. Probability 50%.**

Trade wars. Supply chain ruptures. Shortages cascade.

Regenerative infrastructure becomes survival infrastructure. Not optional. Not ideological.

Those without local food face hunger. Those without local water face thirst.

Preparation: close all four loops. Water. Food. Energy. Waste. Build redundancy. Build resilience.

**Scenario C: Regional Blocs. Probability 30%.**

US. EU. China. Each controls its sphere. Limited trade between spheres.

Bioregional self-sufficiency becomes essential. Inter-bioregion trade is fragile.

Preparation: build complete bioregional systems. Do not depend on inter-regional trade.

**Scenario D: War or Conflict. Probability 10%.**

Resource wars. Territorial conflicts. Strategic competition.

Regenerative communities have strategic value. They produce food. They produce energy. They produce resilient people.

They also have vulnerability. They need defense. They need obscurity.

Preparation: distributed locations. Distributed knowledge. No single point of failure.

Some activities survive all collapse scenarios. These are the highest priority.

**Food production.** Regenerative farming. Closed-loop systems. No external inputs.

**Water systems.** Local water. Rainwater catchment. Filtration. Reuse.

**Energy.** Solar. Thermal. Small-scale. Repairable.

**Practical trades.** Plumbing. Electrical. Welding. Mechanical repair.

**Healthcare.** complete. Local. Low-technology.

**Skills training.** Knowledge transfer. Oral tradition. Documented practice.

**Housing.** Building. Retrofitting. Thermal efficiency.

Activities with lower collapse survival: high-technology manufacturing. Global logistics. Financial services.

Collapse-resilient economics requires no external dependency.

**Time banking.** Survives inflation. Equal exchange of time. One hour equals one hour.

**Mutual credit.** No debt-based money. Credit issued by community. No interest.

**Local production.** Degrow global supply. Produce locally what was imported.

**Skills as assets.** Portable. Tradable. Skills transfer between collapse scenarios.

Scenario A needs gradual transition infrastructure. Build slowly. Build local.

Scenario B needs maximum resilience. Redundancy everywhere. No single points of failure.

Scenario C needs bioregional completeness. Nothing external. Everything local.

Scenario D needs distributed and obscure. No flagship. No target.

The question is not which scenario happens. The question is what survives all scenarios.

What survives: local food. Local water. Local energy. Practical skills. Trust networks.

What fails: global supply chains. Debt-based finance. High-technology dependency. Extractive relationships.

Build for what survives. Let go of what fails.
