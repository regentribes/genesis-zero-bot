# Integral Collective Vii Autonomous Governance Engine

System (metaphor for an integrated whole or network):

This architecture creates a governance system that operates continuously.
Proposals emerge from community discussions.
Decisions execute automatically.
Results are verifiable by all members.

Fusion 1 is Holochain with governance logic.
Source chains record all governance actions.
DHT distributes proposals and votes.
No central server for governance to fail.

Fusion 2 is Solid PODs with identity.
Member credentials in Solid PODs.
ZK proofs verify membership anonymously.
Skills and impact scores attached via credentials.

Fusion 3 is ICP canisters with execution.
Governance decisions execute as canisters.
Resource allocations happen on-chain.
Canisters enforce decision outcomes.

Fusion 4 is x402 with contribution tracking.
Time contributions stream to contributors.
ZK proofs verify contributions without revealing hours.
Impact scores accumulate based on contributions.

Layer 1 is the proposal layer.
Any member can create proposal.
Proposal stored on Holochain DHT.
Discussion happens async and transparent.

Layer 2 is the deliberation layer.
Members comment on proposals.
Comments recorded on source chain.
Deliberation visible to all.

Layer 3 is the voting layer.
Quadratic voting reduces plutocratic capture.
ZK proofs verify vote weight.
Votes recorded on source chain.

Layer 4 is the execution layer.
ICP canisters monitor governance state.
Quorum reached triggers execution.
Resources allocated automatically.

Mechanic 1 is quadratic voting.
Votes cost square of vote count.
Many small votes beat few large votes.
Wealth does not dominate.

Mechanic 2 is ZK vote verification.
Voter proves membership without identity.
Voter proves vote weight without amount.
Voting is private but verifiable.

Mechanic 3 is time-weighted impact.
Longer membership means more trust.
Impact scores amplify vote weight.
Contribution matters more than tenure.

Mechanic 4 is consent gradients.
Unanimous consent for high-impact decisions.
Supermajority for medium decisions.
Simple majority for low-impact.

Type 1 is resource allocation.
Who gets what from shared pool.
Budget distributed by governance.
Allocation executes on ICP.

Type 2 is standard setting.
What counts as regenerative practice.
Standards encoded as UNTP criteria.
Compliance verifiable by all.

Type 3 is conflict resolution.
Disputes between members.
Neutral third party arbitrates.
Outcome enforced by community.

Type 4 is strategic direction.
Long-term community direction.
Vision and values articulated.
Roadmap approved by membership.

Contract 1 is the allocation contract.
Holds community resources.
Distributes based on governance decisions.
Tracks all allocations transparently.

Contract 2 is the contribution contract.
Tracks member contributions.
Issues impact tokens.
Updates ZK verifiable scores.

Contract 3 is the compliance contract.
Verifies regenerative standards.
Issues conformity credentials.
Blocks non-compliant transactions.

Property 1 is legitimacy through participation.
Every member can propose and vote.
Decisions bind all because all participate.
No external authority overrides.

Property 2 is accountability through visibility.
All actions recorded on source chain.
Anyone can audit governance history.
No backroom deals survive scrutiny.

Property 3 is adaptation through iteration.
Failed decisions visible.
Community adjusts and retries.
Learning accumulates over time.

Property 4 is scale through federation.
Communities federate for coordination.
Cross-community governance emerges.
Global coordination from local actions.
