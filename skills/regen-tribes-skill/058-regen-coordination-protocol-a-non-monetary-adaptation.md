# Regen Coordination Protocol A Non Monetary Adaptation

This document takes the Bitcoin mechanism.
It adapts the mechanism for non-monetary coordination.
No coins. No mining. No proof-of-work. No profit.

Bitcoin solves double-spending with economic incentive.
This protocol solves coordination with social trust.
Same mechanisms. Different purpose.

Regenerative communities need coordination without authority.
No central server owns the data.
No corporation mediates disputes.
No bank charges transaction fees.
No government controls the records.

People must agree on what happened.
People must agree on what is true.
Without a central authority to decide.

System (metaphor for an integrated whole or network):

A coordination system based on cryptographic proof.
No trusted third party.
Peers agree directly.
All records are public.
Records cannot be altered retroactively.

Trust comes from the graph of relationships.
Not from the amount of electricity expended.

A claim is a statement made by one person.
A claim has a timestamp.
A claim has a signature from the maker.
A claim has a subject and a predicate.

Example claim.
Alice says. Bob contributed 10 hours to the water project on March 15.

The claim is stored in a local log.
The claim is broadcast to all connected peers.
Peers store the claim in their own logs.

A claim references the previous claim made by the same person.
This forms a personal chain of claims.
Each link has a hash of the previous link.
Each link reinforces all previous links.

If a link is changed all subsequent links are broken.
The chain proves the sequence of what each person said.

Bitcoin uses proof-of-work to order transactions.
This protocol uses proof-of-contribution to order claims.

A contribution is verified by witnesses.
Two or more people confirm that the contribution happened.
Witnesses sign the verification.
Verification is the proof.

Proof-of-contribution is not computational.
It is social.
It requires real relationship.
It requires real observation.

Bitcoin resolves conflicts via longest chain.
Longest chain means most CPU power invested.
This favors whoever has more electricity.

This protocol resolves conflicts via consent.
If two conflicting claims appear the community discusses.
The community decides which claim to accept.
The accepted claim forms the canonical record.

Consent replaces arithmetic.
People replace machines.
Relationship replaces hash rate.

Bitcoin uses hash chains to prove transaction order.
This protocol uses hash chains to prove fact history.

The knowledge graph stores claims.
Each claim links to previous claims about the same subject.
Links form a directed acyclic graph.
The graph shows how knowledge evolved.

A claim about water quality references the sensor reading.
The sensor reading references the calibration.
The calibration references the instrument identity.
All references are cryptographically linked.

Tampering is detectable.
Tampering breaks the chain.
Broken chains are visible to all participants.

A timestamp server timestamps claims.
This is not a single server.
This is a quorum of witnesses.
A quorum is a threshold of signatures.

The quorum approves each new claim.
Approved claims get a collective timestamp.
The timestamp proves the claim existed at that time.

Witnesses are rotated periodically.
No single witness can dominate.
Diversity of witnesses prevents capture.

Bitcoin incentives mining with new coins.
Mining is profitable.
Profit drives participation.
Profit aligns self-interest with network security.

This protocol incentives contribution with social recognition.
Contribution is recorded publicly.
Contribution earns standing.
Standing earns influence.

There is no money to earn.
There is no coin to accumulate.
There is only contribution to the commons.

Contributing is its own reward.
Free riders are visible.
Free riders lose standing.
Standing loss is the cost of not contributing.

Bitcoin resolves disputes by chain length.
Longest valid chain wins.
Economic power decides outcomes.

This protocol resolves disputes by consent process.
Tissue (metaphor for a connected group or network):

Tissue (metaphor for connected group or network): A dispute goes to the tissue council.
The tissue council hears all parties.
The council decides by consent.

If unresolved the dispute escalates.
Organ (metaphor for a functional subsystem or team):

Escalation goes to the organ council.
Organ council decides by consent.
Body (metaphor for the complete collective or organism):

Final escalation goes to the body council.

Decisions are recorded.
Decisions become precedent.
Precedent shapes future decisions.

Claims are stored in a Merkle tree.
Only the root hash is stored in the chain.
Old claims can be pruned to save space.
Pruning removes claims buried under enough confirmations.

What matters is the root.
The root proves the tree existed.
Pruned claims remain verifiable while they are referenced.

Bitcoin requires electricity to function.
This protocol requires relationship to function.

Bitcoin rewards miners with new coins.
This protocol rewards contributors with standing.

Bitcoin resolves conflict with longest chain.
This protocol resolves conflict with consent.

Bitcoin creates money.
This protocol creates trust.

Bitcoin is a financial system.
This protocol is a social system.

They share the same mechanism.
They serve different purposes.

The protocol has four layers.

Layer 1 is the identity layer.
Each participant has a public key pair.
The public key is the identity.
The private key signs claims.

Layer 2 is the log layer.
Each participant maintains a personal log.
Logs sync between peers.
Logs are append-only.
Logs use hash chains.

Layer 3 is the consensus layer.
Witnesses approve new claims.
Quorum signatures provide timestamps.
Consent process resolves disputes.

Layer 4 is the knowledge layer.
Claims feed the knowledge graph.
Relations connect claims to each other.
The graph grows with each verified contribution.

This protocol does not replace Bitcoin.
Bitcoin can be used for external trade.
This protocol manages internal coordination.

External money enters through the exchange layer.
Exchange layer maps external value to internal contribution.
Internal contribution is measured in time.
External value is measured in market price.

The two systems interface at the boundary.
They do not compete.
They serve different functions.

Claim. A statement signed by a participant.

Chain of claims. A personal sequence of claims linked by hash.

Proof-of-contribution. Verification by witnesses that work was done.

Consent-based selection. Dispute resolution through group decision.

Quorum. A threshold of witness signatures that approves a claim.

Merkle tree. Claims hashed pairwise into a root hash for efficient storage.

Witness. A participant who confirms that another contributed.

Standing. Public reputation earned through verified contribution.
