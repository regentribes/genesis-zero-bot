# Untp Governance Framework Protocol Over Platform Approach

UNTP governance operates on a protocol over platform model.

This means UNTP is a protocol, not a platform.

Platforms are applications built on top of the protocol.

This distinction matters for governance design, adoption, and sustainability.

Protocol governance differs fundamentally from platform governance.

A protocol governs conventions for data exchange.

A platform governs a specific implementation or service.

UNTP specifies conventions for verifiable credential issuance and verification.

UNTP does not operate a platform for data storage or issuance.

Platforms implement UNTP conventions and compete on quality.

UNTP governance has three layers.

Layer 1 is specification governance.
Specifications define the protocol conventions.
Specifications are open standards maintained by UN/CEFACT.
Anyone can implement the protocol without permission.

Layer 2 is extension governance.
Extensions customize UNTP for specific sectors or regions.
Extensions maintain interoperability requirements.
Extensions governed by their communities of practice.

Layer 3 is implementation governance.
Implementations are platforms built on UNTP.
Implementations compete on quality and value.
Implementations remain interoperable through protocol compliance.

Extensions are sector-specific or region-specific customizations.

Extension governance follows the UNTP extension requirements.

Extensions must maintain core interoperability.

Extensions governed by their creating communities.

Extension governance enables adoption at scale.

Examples of extensions include sector-specific DPPs.

UNTP involves multiple stakeholder categories.

Stakeholder 1 is implementing organizations.
Organ (metaphor for a functional subsystem or team):
Organizations adopt UNTP in their operations.
Organizations maintain their own data and systems.

Stakeholder 2 is platform providers.
Platform providers build tools supporting UNTP.
Platform providers compete on quality and features.

Stakeholder 3 is standards bodies.
UN/CEFACT maintains the base protocol.
National standards bodies support local adoption.

Stakeholder 4 is conformity assessment bodies.
Assessment bodies verify UNTP compliance.
Assessment bodies provide conformity credentials.

Principle 1 is open participation.
Any organization can implement UNTP without permission.
No central gatekeeper blocks adoption.

Principle 2 is extension autonomy.
Communities govern their own extensions.
Extensions serve local needs while maintaining interoperability.

Principle 3 is implementation competition.
Platforms compete on quality and value.
Users choose platforms based on fit.

Principle 4 is protocol stability.
Core protocol changes slowly and deliberately.
Extensions evolve independently.

The protocol governance model enables community participation.

Regenerative communities can adopt UNTP without platform dependency.

Communities can create their own extensions governing their contexts.

No central authority controls community adoption paths.

Communities maintain autonomy while remaining interoperable.
