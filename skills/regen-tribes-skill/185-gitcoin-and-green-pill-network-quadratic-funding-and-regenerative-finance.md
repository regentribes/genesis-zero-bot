# Gitcoin And Green Pill Network Quadratic Funding And Regenerative Finance

Gitcoin and Green Pill represent two approaches to funding public goods and regenerative outcomes through cryptographic mechanisms.

Gitcoin uses quadratic funding to allocate grants to open-source projects and public goods.

Green Pill uses tokenized incentives and Universal Basic Income (UBI) mechanics to drive regenerative behaviors.

Both operate at the intersection of blockchain technology, governance, and economic design.

Gitcoin is a coordination infrastructure for communities to build, fund, and govern the open internet.

The platform enables quadratic funding rounds where matching pools amplify small individual donations.

The goal is to fund open-source software and public goods that benefit the internet commons.

Gitcoin launched in 2017 as a platform for developers to collaborate on GitHub issues for cryptocurrency.

Early focus was on bug bounties and microtasks for open-source projects.

The platform evolved to focus on quadratic funding grants rounds starting in 2019.

Gitcoin Grants Round 5 in 2020 demonstrated quadratic matching at scale.

COVID-19 Fund in 2020 raised over $1.2M for pandemic relief through quadratic funding.

The platform has facilitated over $60M in direct grants funding through multiple rounds.

Gitcoin DAO launched in 2021 to decentralize governance of the platform.

The platform expanded beyond Ethereum to multi-chain operations.

Quadratic funding (QF) is a mathematical formula that amplifies small donations.

System (metaphor for an integrated whole or network):

The formula mathematically approximates a one-person-one-vote system.

Small donors have disproportionate influence compared to large donors.

A matching pool multiplies donations based on the number of unique donors rather than the total amount.

The Gitcoin QF formula is:
Matching contribution equals the square of the sum of square roots of contributions divided by the sum of contributions.

This means 100 people giving $1 each produces more matching than 1 person giving $100.

Critics note that QF can be gamed through sybil attacks (fake identities).

Gitcoin addresses sybil resistance through identity verification mechanisms.

The Gitcoin Grants Stack provides tooling for running quadratic funding rounds.

Grants Hub is the platform for discovering and donating to grants.

The Stack enables organizations to run their own QF rounds with custom matching pools.

Alpha Governor is governance tooling for on-chain decision making.

Allo Protocol provides the primitive for allocating funds to recipients.

The Stack powers both Gitcoin rounds and external rounds by other organizations.

Gitcoin runs quarterly grants rounds with specific themes.

Each round has a designated matching pool from sponsors.

Rounds have attracted thousands of projects and hundreds of thousands of donors.

Sponsors include protocols, foundations, and individual philanthropists.

RetroPGF is a mechanism pioneered by Optimism for funding public goods retroactively.

Projects are funded based on their proven impact rather than promises.

Gitcoin has incorporated RetroPGF-style rounds into its offerings.

The logic is that it is easier to evaluate past impact than predict future value.

RetroPGF creates incentives for building valuable things without knowing they will be funded.

The tool enables DAOs to create and vote on proposals.

Features include quorum requirements and veto mechanisms.

Used by Gitcoin DAO and external organizations.

The protocol separates funding strategy from recipient selection.

Strategies define how matching occurs (QF, simple matching, direct allocation).

Green Pill aims to create a regenerative economy by tokenizing positive externalities.

The network aligns financial incentives with regenerative outcomes.

The goal is to make doing good financially sustainable.

The network uses a UBI-like token distribution mechanism called RTL.

Green Pill creates a token called G$ (Green Dollar).

G$ is distributed to people who perform regenerative actions.

Actions include community participation, environmental projects, and mutual aid.

The distribution is algorithmic and transparent.

Recipients can use G$ in the local Green Pill economy or convert to other currencies.

RTL stands for Recipient Threshold Level.

RTL is the minimum balance required to receive new token distributions.

This prevents whale accumulation and ensures broad distribution.

Recipients must maintain minimum balances to stay eligible.

The mechanism creates a floor for participant numbers.

Feature 1 is regenerative action rewards.
Token distribution tied to verified regenerative activities.
Activities include environmental monitoring, community service, knowledge sharing.
Verification through community attestation or oracle data.

Feature 2 is local currency mechanics.
G$ operates as a local exchange token.
Within Green Pill ecosystem, G$ has stable value.
Exchange to external currencies at rates.

Feature 3 is community governance.
Token holders vote on what counts as regenerative.
Democratic definition of positive impact.
Flexibility to adapt to local contexts.

Feature 4 is circular economy integration.
G$ can be earned and spent within regenerative ecosystem.
Tools, materials, and services exchanged.
Value circulates within community rather than extracting outward.

The network has multiple layers.

The base layer is the token contract on Polygon.

The distribution layer implements RTL mechanics.

The verification layer rewards regenerative actions.

The governance layer decides eligibility and priorities.
