The problem is tight contamination control gives beneficial effect of product purity and yield.
The harmful factor is high capex and operational complexity and single point of failure.

The physical contradiction is this: the clean room must be simultaneously hermetic to exclude contaminants.
The clean room must be simultaneously accessible for human operators and maintenance and materials flow.

Pharmaceutical aseptic manufacturing operates at ISO 5 or Class 100.
The particulate count is 3520 per cubic meter for particles 0.5 micrometer and larger.
Key requirements are positive pressure and HEPA filters and full gowning and temperature 20 to 24 degrees C and relative humidity 30 to 35 percent.

PCB manufacturing operates at ISO 7 to 8 or Class 10K to 100K.
The particulate count is 352000 to 3520000 per cubic meter.
Key requirements are laminar flow hoods for SMT and basic ESD protection and dust control.

MCU and semiconductor wafer fab operates at ISO 3 to 5.
The particulate count is 35 to 3520 per cubic meter.
Key requirements are ULPA filters and full bunny suits and plus or minus 0.1 degree C temp control and vibration isolation.

EUV lithography targets ISO 1.
The particulate count is 10 per cubic meter.
Key requirements are vacuum environment and hydrogen radical cleaning and molecular contamination control and sub-1nm tolerance.

The key inventive solutions that emerged across manufacturing domains use Altshullers 40 Principles.

Principle 1 is Segmentation.
Applied to Clean Room: divide into isolated zones.
Example: ISO 5 core plus ISO 7 surrounding.
Result: smaller high-clean zone equals less volume to condition.

Principle 2 is Local Quality.
Applied to Clean Room: only the critical zone gets ISO 5.
Peripheral areas get ISO 7 to 8.
Result: reduced energy and complexity by 70 to 80 percent.

Principle 3 is Asymmetry.
Applied to Clean Room: one-way air flow and differential pressure.
Result: directional contamination exclusion without full sealing.

Principle 4 is Self-service or Self-cleaning.
Applied to Clean Room: UV germicidal irradiation and hydrogen radicals and self-disinfecting surfaces.
Result: reduces need for consumable sterilization agents.

Principle 5 is Separation by time.
Applied to Clean Room: intermittent UV cycles during non-production periods.
Result: lower continuous power draw.

Principle 6 is Domaining.
Applied to Clean Room: isolator technology or glove box isolators for pharma.
Result: operator is separated from ISO 5 zone by a physical barrier.

Principle 7 is Prior Action.
Applied to Clean Room: pre-sterilized single-use consumables.
Result: eliminates cleaning infrastructure entirely.

Principle 8 is Staging.
Applied to Clean Room: cascade pressure zones from ISO 7 to ISO 6 to ISO 5.
Result: graduated contamination exclusion versus single clean zone.

The compound is manufactured with zero contamination risk.
Zero cleaning and sterilization infrastructure is needed.
Zero ongoing energy cost is needed.
The process happens using only locally available materials and knowledge.

TRIZ partial solutions moving toward IFR are these.

Solution 1: eliminate the clean room via single-use closed systems.
Pre-sterilized sealed containers are gamma-irradiated at source and assembled in isolated glove boxes.
The clean room becomes unnecessary.

Solution 2: replace active filtration with passive isolation.
Sealed ampoules and blister packs and nitrogen-filled containers create contamination barriers without HEPA infrastructure.

Solution 3: biosynthesis instead of chemical synthesis.
Penicillin-type compounds produced via fermentation require less stringent conditions than synthetic chemistry requiring reactive precursors.
Fermentation is low-tech biology.

Solution 4: botanical pharmacy as fallback.
Plants already manufacture complex alkaloids at ambient conditions.
The clean room problem becomes a cultivation and extraction problem.

Solution 5: point-of-care compounding.
Small-batch on-demand production from precursors uses sealed kit systems similar to homebrew beer brewing kits.
This reduces shelf-life and transport issues.
