# Pix Global Trust Architect Community Governance Model

Pix Global hosts the Trust Architect Community at chat.pix.io.

The community brings together practitioners working on supply chain transparency and UNTP implementation.

The community provides education, resources, and peer connection for trust architects.

The Trust Architect Community serves multiple functions.

Function 1 is education.
Webinars provide ongoing education about UNTP and trust architecture.
Recordings published for asynchronous learning.
Community members access historical content and ongoing sessions.

Function 2 is peer connection.
Members connect with others doing similar work.
Shared resources and experiences accelerate learning.
Peer networks form across geographic and sector boundaries.

Function 3 is resource sharing.
Documentation and tools shared within community.
Best practices documented and propagated.
Case studies from real implementations inform new practitioners.

A trust architect combines business, policy, and technology disciplines.

The trust architect brings together business value creation, community and standards, and technical implementation.

The role requires understanding of all three domains simultaneously.

Trust architects design business cases for organizations and communities.

Trust architects help organizations realize value from UNTP implementation.

Engagement happens through webinar series and ongoing discussions.

Webinars cover policy, business, and technology dimensions.

Sessions include Q&A and interactive discussion.

Recordings available for members who cannot attend live.

Resource 1 is the UNTP website.
unTP.org provides technical documentation.
Reference implementations available on GitHub.
Specs and requirements published openly.

Resource 2 is the Pix Global website.
pix.io provides general resources.
chat.pix.io hosts the community platform.
Slides and materials shared through community channels.

Resource 3 is YouTube.
Recordings of webinars published for broader audience.
Topics span policy, business, and technology.
Series accessible without community membership.

The community operates with open participation.

Practitioners self-identify as trust architects.

No formal certification required to participate.

Community reputation built through contributions and expertise demonstrated in discussions.

Knowledge flows through peer learning rather than top-down instruction.

The community supports UNTP adoption globally.

Trust architects use community resources to implement UNTP in their contexts.

Community provides feedback loop to UNTP development process.

Practitioners inform protocol evolution through implementation experience.
