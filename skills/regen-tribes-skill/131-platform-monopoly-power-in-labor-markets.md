# Platform Monopoly Power In Labor Markets

A single platform holding job discovery monopoly means:

- Any rule change propagates across the entire market. Not just that platform.
- Employers using the platform must comply, or they lose access to the candidate pool.
- Job seekers have no alternative venue. They are captive.
- The platforms rule changes become de facto labor market regulations with no democratic input.

Government databases are designed for tax and legal administration, not career representation.

Contract work produces 1099s, scattered, inconsistent.
Self-employment often has minimal or no formal record.
International gigs are fragmented across jurisdictions.
Creative and skill work is invisible in tax systems.
Community and organization roles have no bureaucratic footprint.

System (metaphor for an integrated whole or network):

When a platform requires government database verification, it maps careers onto a system designed for taxation. Not for capability. The match is imperfect at best, destructive at worst.

1. Platform introduces rule. Experience verification via government databases.
2. Employers adopt. No choice, candidate pool is captive.
3. Candidates filtered. Those who do not fit lose access.
4. Market adjusts to new normal.
5. Non-compliant candidates are locked out of all employers using the platform.

Monopolies do not face competitive pressure to maintain fairness.

A platform with 80% market share can introduce harmful rule changes. Job seekers have nowhere else to go.

Critical infrastructure requires democratic governance, not private platform rule.

Regenerative communities must build alternative discovery networks that are not captive to any single platform.
The strategy: maintain presence on multiple platforms, build direct hiring channels, create community-owned job matching infrastructure.

The goal is to make the platform a convenience, not a dependency.
