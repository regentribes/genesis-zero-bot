# Integral Collective Iv Regenerative Knowledge Commons

System (metaphor for an integrated whole or network):

This architecture builds a community knowledge system where data, relationships, and learning form a living commons.
The system captures, connects, and evolves community knowledge over time.

Fusion 1 is RDF knowledge graphs with Solid PODs.
Community knowledge stored as RDF triples.
Solid PODs give individuals control over their contributions.
WebID links knowledge to people without exposing identity.

Fusion 2 is Holochain source chains with knowledge graphs.
Every knowledge addition has an immutable author record.
Source chain provides attribution without central authority.
DHT distributes knowledge across peer nodes.

Fusion 3 is SurrealDB as query engine.
Unified query across documents, vectors, and RDF graphs.
SurrealQL provides powerful retrieval.
Time-series captures knowledge evolution over time.

Fusion 4 is auKsys methodology for knowledge processing.
Acquire from sensors and humans.
Process with algorithms to extract meaning.
Store in RDF with provenance.
Share across community with access control.

Layer 1 is the observation layer.
IoT sensors record environmental conditions.
Humans contribute observations via Solid POD.
Observations stored as RDF assertions.

Layer 2 is the pattern layer.
SurrealDB identifies patterns in observations.
Vector similarity finds related concepts.
Graph relationships emerge from pattern analysis.

Layer 3 is the model layer.
Patterns combine into predictive models.
Models predict outcomes of interventions.
Models validated against new observations.

Layer 4 is the wisdom layer.
Models inform community decisions.
Decisions create new observations.
Cycle continues and knowledge grows.

Type 1 is ecological knowledge.
Soil health observations linked to outcomes.
Plant growth patterns across seasons.
Water cycle relationships mapped.

Type 2 is social knowledge.
How community decisions form.
Who contributes what expertise.
Relationship patterns that strengthen community.

Type 3 is technical knowledge.
How to build and repair things.
Where resources are located.
Who has what skills.

Type 4 is historical knowledge.
What worked and what failed.
Why previous decisions were made.
Lessons from past seasons.

Control 1 is public knowledge.
Anyone can read and query.
Community benefits from open sharing.
Examples include weather patterns and plant varieties.

Control 2 is contributor knowledge.
Read by all, written by contributor.
Attribution preserved via Holochain source chain.
Examples include personal observations and learnings.

Control 3 is group knowledge.
Shared by working groups and circles.
Groups define their own access policies.
Examples include project plans and group decisions.

Control 4 is private knowledge.
Encrypted in Solid POD.
Only owner can decrypt.
Examples include personal notes and drafts.

Interface 1 is semantic search.
Natural language queries via SurrealDB vectors.
Context-aware results based on user role.
Privacy filtering respects access controls.

Interface 2 is graph exploration.
Navigate knowledge relationships visually.
Discover unexpected connections.
Follow provenance trails back to sources.

Interface 3 is temporal analysis.
How knowledge evolved over time.
What patterns emerged when.
Knowledge freshness visible to users.

Integration 1 is with community education.
Learning paths stored as knowledge graph.
Progress tracked via Solid POD credentials.
Skills verified via ZK skill credentials.

Integration 2 is with mentorship.
Expertise identified via knowledge contributions.
Mentors matched to learners via graph analysis.
Learning outcomes feed back into knowledge.

Integration 3 is with documentation.
Community practices documented as knowledge.
Documentation improves through use.
Living documents evolve with community.
