# The Book That Disappeared-10-Years Of Research Notes

This note captures the insight that books disappear when authors stop publishing.

Books disappear from collective knowledge. Authors write. Authors publish. Authors stop.

When authors stop publishing, books fade. Libraries stop carrying them. Search engines stop indexing them. References dry up.

The knowledge does not disappear. The knowledge moves underground. People share copies. Copies diverge. Divergence produces noise.

The noise grows over decades. Eventually no one remembers the original.

The pattern repeats across domains. Medical research disappears. Political analysis disappears. Technical knowledge disappears.

Disappearance happens when institutions collapse. Institutions supporting the work dissolve. The work loses its home.

Disappearance happens when authors move on. Authors retire. Authors die. Authors simply stop caring.

Disappearance happens when platforms change. Platforms shut down. Platforms change terms. Content becomes inaccessible.

The Living Book addresses disappearance. Living Book preserves by design.

Preservation requires active copying. Active copying distributes the work. Distributed work survives platform changes.

Preservation requires attribution. Attribution links contributors. Links enable compensation. Compensation motivates preservation.

Preservation requires update. Update keeps the work relevant. Relevant work stays in use. Use drives preservation.

Preservation is not passive. Passive preservation fails. Archives fail. Libraries fail. Digital archives fail.

Active preservation requires resources. Resources require funding. Funding requires value. Value requires use.

The cycle is self-reinforcing. Use drives preservation. Preservation enables use.

The Living Book creates this cycle. The cycle creates longevity.

Community preserves what matters to community. Community funds preservation. Community updates the work.

External institutions do not preserve community knowledge. External institutions have their own priorities.

Community knowledge requires community infrastructure. Infrastructure requires community investment. Investment requires trust.

Trust requires shared ownership. Shared ownership prevents extraction. Prevention enables investment.

RegenTribes knowledge must survive. Surviving requires active preservation.

Active preservation means publishing to Living Book. Publishing means attribution. Attribution means contribution tracking.

Contribution tracking means the knowledge graph knows who added what. Knows when. Knows why.

The graph enables discovery. Discovery enables use. Use enables preservation.

The book that disappeared was probably good. Good books disappear. Mediocre books survive.

Survival requires ongoing effort. Ongoing effort requires motivation. Motivation requires value.

Value requires relevance. Relevance requires update. Update requires investment.

The books that survive are not the best. They are the most maintained.

The Living Book inverts this. The Living Book rewards maintenance. Maintenance produces quality. Quality produces survival.

Publish to Living Book. Publish means contribute. Contribute means tracked.

Tracked means attributed. Attributed means compensated. Compensated means motivated.

Motivated means maintained. Maintained means preserved.

The cycle starts with one act. The act is publishing.
