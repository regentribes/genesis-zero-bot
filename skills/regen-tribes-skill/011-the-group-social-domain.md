# The Group Social Domain

Tissue (metaphor for a connected group or network):

Tissue (metaphor for connected group or network): A tissue is a small group.
Three to twelve people.
A household or a core team.

Trust is the foundation.
Everything else builds on it.

Trust grows through repeated interaction.
You do what you say. Others see.
Keeping small promises builds trust.
Breaking small promises erodes trust.

The first trust act is keeping a small promise.
Nothing more.
Trust builds slowly.
Trust breaks quickly.

Roles emerge from what people actually do.
Roles do not emerge from what people say they will do.
Assign roles by observation.
Watch who does what well.
Ask them to do more of it.

Consent is the decision method.
A decision passes when no one blocks it.

Blocking is serious.
Blocking means the proposed action would harm the blocker.
Blocking requires a proposal everyone can live with.
Not everyone loves it. Just lives with it.

Consent is not agreement.
Agreement means yes.
Consent means no blocking.

Time banking measures contribution.
One hour equals one time credit.
All work counts. Cooking. Cleaning. Repair. Teaching. Listening.

Time credits do not earn interest.
This is the rule. No exceptions.

Time credits have a floor.
You never go below zero.
The community tops you up.

Time credits have a ceiling.
You cannot accumulate beyond 200 hours per year.
The ceiling prevents hoarding. The floor prevents poverty.

Conflict is normal.
It is a signal.
It means something needs attention.

The conflict process: name the issue. Hear all sides. Propose. Test.

If unresolved get a third party.
Someone outside the group.
They do not decide. They help the group decide.

Meetings have a format.
Check-in. Agenda. Discussion. Decision. Check-out.
Check-in means one sentence on how you feel.
Check-out means one sentence on what you got.

This format takes 15 minutes.
It prevents most meeting problems.

Decision logs are written.
Who. When. Why.
This is memory.

Memory prevents the same arguments again and again.
When someone leaves the group keeps functioning.
Decision logs remain.
This is the key advantage over informal groups.

A tissue has a guide. Not a leader.
The guide holds the process.
The guide does not make decisions.
The guide makes sure decisions get made well.
