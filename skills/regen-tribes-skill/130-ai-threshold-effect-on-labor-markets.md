# Ai Threshold Effect On Labor Markets

When one human plus AI equals multiple unaugmented humans on standard tasks, the equilibrium price does not just adjust. It collapses.

Supply of capable output multiplies. One augmented worker equals many unaugmented workers. Demand for the unaugmented version evaporates. Price per unit drops to match the new supply reality.

Standard tasks have a market price.
When AI-augmented individuals can produce that task at one-fifth the cost per unit, the market price adjusts downward.
What used to be worth X dollars per hour becomes worth X divided by five.
Not because the task changed, but because the supply of capable output multiplied.

Code generation. Junior developer value compressed.

Content creation. Per-piece rates in freefall.

Legal document review. Per-document pricing collapsing.

Standard analytics. Automated dashboards replacing analyst hours.

The threshold is crossed, not approaching. Multiple categories have already crossed the line.

The market has not fully adjusted yet. There is lag between capability and price. But the direction is set.

Specialists in these categories face a trap.
They compete against their own augmented peers.
Retraining to work with AI is necessary but not sufficient.
The augmented baseline keeps rising.
Todays AI-augmented is tomorrows AI-minimal.

The core extraction is this: taking human capability, amplifying it via AI, returning less to humans as value flows upward.

Regenerative communities must build systems where the amplification benefit stays local.
Where AI augmentation serves community goals rather than external capital.
Where the productivity gains build common wealth rather than private returns.

The alternative is communities that depend on external AI systems that extract value upward. The regenerative path is building augmentation capacity inside the community fabric itself.
