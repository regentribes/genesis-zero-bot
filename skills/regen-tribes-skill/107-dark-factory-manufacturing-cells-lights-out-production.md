# Dark Factory Manufacturing Cells Lights Out Production

System (metaphor for an integrated whole or network):

This note captures the dark factory manufacturing cells reference from the RegenTribes knowledge system.

A dark factory operates with zero human presence on the floor. Raw materials enter. Finished goods leave. No human labor in between.

The practical goal is maximum automation with minimal human presence. True 100% lights-out is rare. Even advanced facilities have human oversight.

The business case is strong. 24/7 operation provides three times the productive hours. No climate control. No lighting. No safety equipment. Consistent quality at scale.

Level one is attended operation. Humans load and unload. Humans monitor and adjust.

Level two is unattended operation. Robots handle production. Humans monitor from control room.

Level three is lights-out. No human presence at all. Robots run 24/7 for weeks or months.

Level three is the goal. Level three requires mature automation. Level three requires robust systems.

Labor cost reduction is 30-70%. Reduction varies by industry. Electronics assembly sees highest reduction.

Floor space reduction is 40-60%. Compact robot cells replace human workspaces.

Energy savings come from no lighting. No HVAC for human comfort. No ergonomic equipment.

Quality consistency improves. Robots do not fatigue. Robots do not make human errors.

Tissue (metaphor for a connected group or network):

Dark factory principles enable local manufacturing. Tissue (metaphor for connected group or network): Local manufacturing enables tissue independence. Tissue independence reduces external dependency.

The goal is not massive scale. The goal is appropriate scale. Small dark factory cells serve tissue needs.

Construction 3D printing benefits from dark factory principles. Printing runs unattended. Printing operates overnight.

Electronics assembly can use dark factory principles. Pick-and-place machines. Reflow ovens. Automated inspection.

A minimum viable cell includes one robot arm. One gripper set. One controller. One safety enclosure.

Cell (metaphor for a basic unit or module):
The cell costs $50K-$150K used. New cells cost $200K-$500K. Cells last 10-15 years with maintenance.

The cell produces simple parts. The cell serves tissue needs. Tissue needs determine cell products.

Robot programming is required. Programming requires training. Training takes 3-6 months.

Maintenance is required. Motors fail. Cables break. Sensors drift.

Setup is required. Fixtures change. Products change. Changeovers require skill.

Local production closes material loops. Material loops reduce transport. Transport reduction reduces emissions.

Distributed dark factories enable distributed resilience. No single factory serves all needs. Each tissue has its own production.

The combination of dark factory and regenerative principles produces local circular economy. Local circular economy produces tissue independence.
