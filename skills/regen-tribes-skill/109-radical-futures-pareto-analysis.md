# Radical Futures Pareto Analysis

This note captures the radical futures Pareto analysis for regenerative community planning.

A radical future is defined as a transition that changes the rules. Not incremental change. Not marginal improvement.

A radical future changes what is possible. What was impossible becomes routine.

Three scenarios drive the analysis. Current trajectory continues. Deglobalization proceeds. Civilizational reset.

Each scenario has a probability. Each probability shapes planning. Planning must account for all three.

The current trajectory continues. Technology advances. Climate degrades. Inequality grows.

Technology continues advancing. Artificial intelligence automates knowledge work. Robotics automates physical work. Biotechnology extends human capability.

Climate continues degrading. Temperatures rise. Weather extremes increase. Agricultural zones shift.

Inequality continues growing. Capital concentrates. Labor displaces. Power consolidates.

System (metaphor for an integrated whole or network):

Planning for continuation means building resilience within the existing system.

Deglobalization proceeds. Supply chains shorten. Regional blocs form. Trade declines.

Deglobalization is already underway. Trade volumes peaked in 2023. Regional trade agreements multiply.

Supply chains shorten. Companies near-shore. Nations stockpile. Reserves grow.

Regional blocs form. US sphere. EU sphere. China sphere. Limited inter-sphere trade.

Planning for deglobalization means building local self-sufficiency. Local food. Local energy. Local manufacturing.

Civilizational reset occurs. Systemic collapse. Power vacuum. Reconstitution.

Reset is possible. Financial system stress. Resource depletion. Climate tipping points.

Reset is not inevitable. Preparation reduces probability. Preparation enables recovery.

Planning for reset means building survival capacity. Seeds. Tools. Skills. Community.

The Pareto frontier identifies actions that perform well across all scenarios.

Actions on the frontier are highest priority. These actions survive any future.

Actions off the frontier are scenario-dependent. These actions require probability estimates.

The frontier is where to focus. Focus maximizes expected value.

Priority one is food production. Food production survives all scenarios. Everyone needs food.

Priority two is water systems. Water is essential. Water systems require local infrastructure.

Priority three is energy independence. Energy enables everything else. Solar and storage provide independence.

Priority four is practical skills. Skills transfer across scenarios. Plumbing. Electrical. Mechanical. Medical.

Priority five is community formation. Community enables cooperation. Cooperation enables survival.

High technology manufacturing is scenario-dependent. It requires global supply chains. It requires stable power.

Digital infrastructure is scenario-dependent. It requires connectivity. It requires maintenance.

Financial systems are scenario-dependent. They require trust. Trust requires institutions.

These actions are lower priority. They are valuable in scenarios A and B. They are useless in scenario C.

Actions on the frontier compound. Food production improves nutrition. Better nutrition improves capability. Better capability improves food production.

Community formation improves trust. Trust improves cooperation. Cooperation improves community formation.

The compounding effect amplifies priority actions. Small investments grow large over time.

The time horizon matters. Actions that pay off in 10 years differ from actions that pay off in 1 year.

Short-term actions are more certain. Long-term actions have more use.

The combination matters. Short-term actions fund long-term actions. Long-term actions define the direction.

The future is not determined. The future is chosen. Choices made now shape probabilities.

Building on the frontier shapes probabilities toward resilience. Resilience enables more choices.

The goal is not prediction. The goal is preparation. Preparation enables agency.
