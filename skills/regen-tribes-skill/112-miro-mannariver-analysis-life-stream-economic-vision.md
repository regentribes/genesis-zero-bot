# Miro Mannariver Analysis Life Stream Economic Vision

This note captures Miro's analysis of the MANNARiver / Life Stream economic vision from the RegenTribes conversation.

The vision is life as the measure. Not money. Not time. Life itself is the unit of account.

System (metaphor for an integrated whole or network):

Ecosystem (metaphor for an interconnected network):

Resources flow to sustain life. Life includes human life. Ecosystem (metaphor for system or network): Life includes ecosystem life. Life includes all living systems.

The flow is continuous. Like a river. Rivers flow to valleys. Valleys sustain life. Life sustains the river.

The circularity is the key. Input is life. Output is life. Waste becomes input.

MANNARiver is an economic design. The design uses life metrics. Life metrics replace money metrics.

MANNARiver measures impact. Impact measures what life receives. What life receives determines value.

Value flows upstream. Upstream contributors enable downstream flourishing. Upstream receives the benefit.

The river metaphor is apt. Rivers carve valleys. Valleys sustain civilizations. Civilizations sustain rivers.

The flow has four stages. Stage one is extraction. Extraction removes from nature. Nature provides resources.

Stage two is production. Production transforms resources. Transformation adds value. Value is life-enhancing.

Stage three is distribution. Distribution delivers to life. Delivery requires infrastructure. Infrastructure serves distribution.

Stage four is regeneration. Regeneration returns to nature. Nature receives back. Nature sustains extraction.

The cycle closes. Closure enables sustainability. Sustainability enables flourishing.

Life metrics measure what matters. What matters is what sustains life.

Carbon footprint is one metric. Carbon measures atmospheric impact. Atmospheric impact affects climate.

Water usage is one metric. Water measures consumption. Consumption affects ecosystems.

Biodiversity impact is one metric. Biodiversity measures ecosystem health. Health measures sustainability.

Energy intensity is one metric. Energy measures resource use. Resource use affects planetary systems.

Distribution is the hardest part. Getting resources to where life needs them is complex.

Centralized distribution is efficient. Efficiency reduces cost. Cost reduction increases access.

Centralized distribution is vulnerable. Vulnerability creates single points of failure. Failures disrupt life.

Distributed distribution is resilient. Resilience enables continuity. Continuity sustains life.

The design must balance efficiency and resilience. Efficiency enables access. Resilience enables sustainability.

Community implementation starts local. Local production uses local resources. Local resources sustain local life.

Local distribution is simple. Simple distribution reduces cost. Cost reduction increases access.

Local regeneration is direct. Direct regeneration closes loops. Closure enables sustainability.

The community becomes a node. Nodes connect to other nodes. Connections form the river.

Governance determines flow. Flow determines life. Life determines value.

Governance must be distributed. Distributed governance prevents capture. Capture concentrates power.

Governance must be accountable. Accountability ensures flow. Flow serves life.

The combination is rare. Most systems improve for one. The vision improves for both.

The time horizon is multi-generational. Multi-generational thinking changes everything.

Short-term thinking extracts. Extraction depletes. Depletion forecloses the future.

Long-term thinking regenerates. Regeneration restores. Restoration enables the future.

The river metaphor works here. Rivers cut canyons over millennia. Millennia require patience.

The political dimension is unavoidable. Any system that changes what counts as value changes power.

Current power benefits from current metrics. Changing metrics changes power.

Organ (metaphor for a functional subsystem or team):
The transition requires political will. Will requires organization. Organization requires trust.

Trust requires community. Community requires time. Time requires patience.

Tissue (metaphor for a connected group or network):

Tissue (metaphor for connected group or network): RegenTribes provides the tissue structure. MANNARiver provides the flow structure.

Tissue structure enables local governance. Flow structure enables regional coordination.

The combination enables planetary stewardship. Planetary stewardship is the goal.

The goal is not improvement. The goal is flourishing. Flourishing is life living fully.
