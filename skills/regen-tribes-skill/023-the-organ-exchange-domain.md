# The Organ Exchange Domain

Organ (metaphor for a functional subsystem or team):

Exchange at the organ level means goods and services and time moving between people.

Time banking is the base unit.
One hour equals one time credit.
All work counts. Cooking. Cleaning. Repair. Teaching. Listening.

Time credits do not earn interest.
This is the rule. No exceptions.

Time credits have a floor.
You never go below zero. The community tops you up.

Time credits have a ceiling.
You cannot accumulate beyond 200 hours per year.
The ceiling prevents hoarding. The floor prevents poverty.

The organ runs a surplus store.
Members add what they grow. Members add what they make. Members add what they find.

Other members take from the surplus store.
They pay with time credits.

Surplus goes to neighboring organs.
What is not taken locally goes externally.

The council tracks exchanges.
It makes sure no zone always gives and never receives.

Balance is reviewed quarterly.
A zone that gives more gets recognition.
A zone that receives more gets a support plan.
No shame. Just help.

External trade follows the same rules.
External goods get priced in time credit equivalent.
Pricing reflects labor time plus materials.

No rent. No interest. No debt.
These are anti-extraction rules.

If someone needs something the organ provides it.
The person does not owe.

What matters is contribution.
What you give to the commons. Not what you hoard.

External money is tracked.
It does not become the base unit. Time credits stay primary.
