# Eco Housing Tower Cluster Financial Model

This note captures the financial model for an eco-housing tower cluster.

Photovoltaic generation was corrected. Original was 184k kWh. Corrected is 145k kWh.

Realistic operating expenditure categories were added. Proper payback calculation with net operating income was added.

Fish yield assumption was flagged. The assumption is aggressive. Production may be lower.

OPEX is operating expenditure. OPEX is the cost of running the systems.

NOI is net operating income. NOI is revenue minus OPEX. NOI excludes debt service.

PV is photovoltaic. PV is solar generation.

LED is light-emitting diode. LED provides grow lighting.

The cluster includes a tower for microgreens. Hydroponic racks. LED grow lights. Climate control.

System (metaphor for an integrated whole or network):

The cluster includes a fish system. Tilapia production. Waste water feeds aquaponics.

The cluster includes rooftop solar. PV panels on roof. 145k kWh annual generation.

The cluster includes battery storage. Lithium pack. 50kWh capacity.

The cluster includes a water system. Rainwater catchment. Filtration. Reuse for aquaponics.

OPEX includes seeds. Seed cost is low. Seeds produce microgreens. Microgreens generate revenue.

OPEX includes fish feed. Feed is the main cost. Feed cost varies with fish weight.

OPEX includes labor. Labor maintains systems. Labor time is seasonal.

OPEX includes energy. Solar reduces grid dependence. Battery provides resilience.

OPEX includes maintenance. Pumps fail. Lights burn out. Filters clog. Maintenance is ongoing.

Revenue comes from microgreens. Microgreens sell to restaurants. Restaurants pay premium. Premium reflects quality.

Revenue comes from fish. Tilapia sells to community. Community consumes locally. Local reduces transport cost.

Revenue comes from energy export. Excess solar exports to grid. Export generates credit.

Revenue comes from education. Workshops teach aquaponics. Participants pay for learning.

Payback uses NOI. NOI is revenue minus OPEX. NOI excludes debt service.

The calculation requires realistic revenue. Revenue assumes market access. Market access requires relationships.

The calculation requires realistic costs. Costs include labor. Labor has opportunity cost.
