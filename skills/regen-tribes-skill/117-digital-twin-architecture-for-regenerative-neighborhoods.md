# Digital Twin Architecture For Regenerative Neighborhoods

System (metaphor for an integrated whole or network):

A coherent digital twin is not a 3D model of a building. It is a reflective substrate that holds all system dimensions as executable, queryable, visualizable graph topology.

Layer 1: DATA INGESTION
IoT sensors, social signals, AME listening.
Raw input normalized into atom types.

Layer 2: SPATIAL / GEOGRAPHIC
Geolocation, land use, topology.
Where things are constrains what they can do.

Layer 3: PHYSICAL INFRASTRUCTURE
Water, energy, food, waste flows.
Circular systems as metabolic topology.

Layer 4: SOCIAL DYNAMICS
FOT indicators, affinity mapping.
Trust topology, betrayal repulsion, regeneration clustering.

Layer 5: KNOWLEDGE GRAPH
Semantic vectors plus NARS truth values.
Holds what-is-known versus known-uncertain versus unknown.

Layer 6: INFERENCE ENGINE
Probabilistic reasoning, PLN, NARS.
Closes loops under noise.

Layer 7: VISUAL INTERFACE
WebGPU instanced rendering.
The rendered scene IS the interface.

Each layer is a node type in the metagraph. Cross-layer edges carry causal flow.

Social trust leads to energy coop participation. Infrastructure failure leads to social stress. The twin runs inference across all layers simultaneously.

Most digital twins fail to bridge social and physical systems.

Social systems model: community trust score.
Physical systems model: water reuse percentage.

The missing link: HOW do they causally influence each other?

The coherence thesis: Trust is not soft. In regenerative neighborhoods, social cohesion directly enables or disables physical infrastructure adoption.

Low FOT leads to residents bypassing circular water systems. Entropy increases. Infrastructure fails faster. Social stress increases. The loop is real. The twin must model it.

FOT 5-indicator composite becomes edge weight function in social graph.

Circular system adoption rates become edge weight function in physical graph.

Cross-layer coupling: social trust edges weighted by infrastructure participation. Infrastructure health edges weighted by social cohesion metrics.

The goal is one queryable graph where social and physical are not separate models but continuous edges in one topology.
