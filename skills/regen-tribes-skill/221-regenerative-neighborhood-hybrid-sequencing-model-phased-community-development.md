# Regenerative Neighborhood Hybrid Sequencing Model Phased Community Development

The Hybrid Sequencing Model combines three time approaches to create a realistic development framework.

Approach 1 is conditions-based. Activities have clear prerequisites but flexible timing.

Approach 2 is integration gates. Major decisions require meeting specific criteria before proceeding.

Approach 3 is coherence spirals. Iterative cycles of build, learn, and improve deepen the outcome.

Every concept is described by its first principles.

Do reversible things first. Only make irreversible decisions when you must, and only after thorough validation.

Irreversible decisions include land purchase, construction contracts, and legal entity formation.

Reversible activities include vision exploration, team alignment, market research, and design iteration.

This sequencing reduces risk by ensuring reversible validation happens before irreversible commitment.

Spiral 1 is Proof-of-Concept. Months 0 to 6. Goal is validating that the vision is possible.

Spiral 2 is Feasibility Validation. Months 3 to 12. Goal is finding specific land and clarifying the capital pathway.

Spiral 3 is Capital improvement. Months 9 to 15. Goal is securing capital and finalizing design.

Spiral 4 is Construction and Operation. Months 12 to 36. Goal is building and occupying the community.

Spiral 5 is Continuous Regeneration. Year 2 onward. Goal is measuring impact and improving.

Spirals overlap intentionally. Work in later spirals begins before earlier spirals complete.

The most realistic timeline from idea to occupancy is 3 to 4 years.

Factor 1 is experienced facilitator. Someone who has built community before guides the process.

Factor 2 is clear governance. Decisions are made fast and not debated endlessly.

Factor 3 is realistic timeline expectations. Land search cannot be rushed.

Factor 4 is strong market validation. Evidence exists that people actually want this.

Factor 5 is early culture seeding. Community culture building begins immediately.

Factor 6 is continuous feedback. After each sprint, pause and learn before proceeding.

Gates are integration points where specific conditions must be met before proceeding.

Gate 1 is Core Team Coherence. Exit criteria include signed values statement, agreed decision-making approach, and clear financial commitments.

Gate 2 is Feasibility Landscape. Exit criteria include 3 to 5 viable bioregions, market validation with 10 or more interested parties, and rough financial model showing required capital.

Gate 3 is Land and Team and Capital Convergence. Exit criteria include land under control, specialist roles filled, and capital pathways identified.

Gate 5 is Capital Assembled and Leadership Ready. Exit criteria include 70 percent or more of capital secured, operational manuals drafted, and master plan complete.

The model provides the project management framework for implementing regenerative community governance.

The four-layer governance framework provides the organizational design. This model provides the timeline and sequencing.
