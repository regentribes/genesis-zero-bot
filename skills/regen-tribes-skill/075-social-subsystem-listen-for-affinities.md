# Social Subsystem Listen For Affinities

The social subsystem connects people without extraction.

It surfaces affinity. It measures trust. It enables coordination.

It does not surveil. It does not store individual behaviors. It does not score people.

The design constraint: listen FOR affinities, not extract FROM people.

**Affinity Mapping Engine.** Surfaces natural attraction patterns. Who wants to work with whom. Who resonates.

**Field of Trust.** Measures trust emergence. Evidence divided by claims. Dynamic ratio.

**Social Knowledge Graph.** Stores collective social knowledge. Emergent patterns only. No individual dossiers.

The engine observes group signals. It does not ask people to report on each other.

Traditional management extracts compliance. It asks: who did what, when, how well.

This creates resistance. People improve for the extractor. The data becomes fake.

The engine observes. Language patterns. Gesture patterns. Choice patterns. Proximity patterns. Time allocation patterns.

Signal types include language patterns. Word choice reveals interest. Frequency reveals priority.

Signal types include gesture patterns. Eye contact reveals connection. Energy reveals engagement.

Signal types include choice patterns. Who chooses to work with whom. Choice of partners reveals affinity.

Signal types include proximity patterns. Who stands near whom. Proximity reveals trust.

Signal types include time allocation. Where time goes reveals what matters.

The engine weights signals by frequency. By intensity. By consistency.

It produces an affinity map. The map shows cluster structure. The map shows strength of connection.

Trust equals evidence divided by claims.

Evidence is what you do. Claims are what you say.

High evidence over claims means high trust. Low evidence over claims means low trust.

High claims over evidence means reputation inflation.

Trust is not binary. It is a ratio. The ratio shifts with every action.

Every interaction updates the ratio. Trust is dynamic. Trust is measurable. Trust is comparable.

The FOT composite is the minimum of five indicators.

Indicator 1 is reliability. Do you do what you say. Do you show up. Do you deliver.

Indicator 2 is competence. Can you do what you claim. Do you have the skill. The knowledge.

Indicator 3 is integrity. Do you say what you mean. Do you mean what you say.

Indicator 4 is consistency. Do you behave the same over time. Across contexts.

Indicator 5 is openness. Do you share information. Mistakes. Learning.

This is the hologram principle. One weak indicator drags the whole field down.

Concentrix differs from concentric.

Concentric means circles sharing the same center. One center. Hierarchical radial.

Concentrix means multiple centers. Polycentric co-centering. Many centers. All equally valid.

Each person has their own trust center. Each person has their own trust radius.

The group trust field is the intersection. People outside the field are not trusted.

They can enter by producing evidence. People inside can exit if trust breaks.

Trust is renewable. Trust is revisable.

The social subsystem stores aggregate patterns. Not individual behaviors.

It answers: what is the group affinity structure. Not: what did person X do to person Y.

It answers: what is the field of trust. Not: what is person X's trust score.

It answers: who naturally clusters around shared work. Not: who has conflicts with whom.

Members can disable the engine at any time. Disabling means the engine ignores you.

Disabling does not erase history. History is aggregate. There is no individual history to erase.

This is not surveillance infrastructure. It is pattern recognition infrastructure.

The social subsystem is Layer 6 in the six-layer architecture.

Layer 1 is Physical. Hardware. Sensors. Actuators.

Layer 2 is Computational. Processing. Memory. Networks.

Layer 3 is Coordination. CDS. Decision processes. Role assignment.

Layer 4 is Economic. ITC. Value exchange. Resource allocation.

Layer 5 is Knowledge. OAD. Ownership. FRS. Knowledge commons.

Layer 6 is Social. Affinity Engine. FOT. Social Knowledge Graph.

Each layer is necessary. Each layer enables the layer above.

Social infrastructure enables coordination. Coordination enables exchange. Exchange enables knowledge sharing.

Without social infrastructure the other layers collapse into hierarchy.

The social subsystem integrates with the simulation layer.

Agents in the simulation have trust values. These values emerge from agent behavior.

The values feed back into the social graph. The graph influences agent assignment.

Human community members see the same affinity patterns. The system is transparent to humans and agents alike.

The social subsystem integrates with the knowledge graph.
