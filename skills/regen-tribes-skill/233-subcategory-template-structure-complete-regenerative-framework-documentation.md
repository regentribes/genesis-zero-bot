# Subcategory Template Structure Complete Regenerative Framework Documentation

Each subcategory in the Regenerative Neighborhood Framework follows a standardized template structure.

The template ensures comprehensive coverage and comparability across all subcategories.

This field covers what challenge the subcategory addresses, why it matters in regenerative neighborhoods, and climate and geographic context variations that affect applicability.

This field covers core values for the subcategory, key outcomes being improved for, and alignment with ecology.

Multiple solution options are presented.
Each approach includes description and core concept, best suited for including climate and scale and economic model, key benefits and trade-offs, capital requirements including upfront costs, ongoing costs and maintenance, community size requirements, implementation timeline, service providers and experts needed, case studies and real examples, key risks and mitigation strategies, and performance metrics.

This field covers dependencies on other systems, feedback loops with other subcategories, how this category informs others, and sequencing implications.

This field covers design tools, monitoring systems, improvement platforms, data collection methods, and simulation tools.

This field covers permitting requirements by region and country, compliance standards, environmental regulations, building codes, labor laws if applicable, and certification programs.

This field covers capital expenditure ranges by approach, operating expenditure breakdown, financing options, revenue potential, cost-benefit analysis, payback periods, and lifecycle costs.

This field covers academic research, video tutorials, open-source designs, project templates, community examples, expert practitioners, online courses, and books and papers.

Evidence levels are proposed for theoretical unproven approaches, emerging for small pilot implementations, proven for multiple implementations, and mature for established practice.

This field covers source organization, expertise credentials, track record, potential conflicts of interest, and community testimonials.

This field covers cost barriers, skill and knowledge requirements, and other obstacles to adoption.

The community applies this template to any new domain not yet documented.

When considering a new technology or approach, the community works through each template field.

This ensures the decision is made with full information rather than partial understanding.
