# Community Note Integral Gtm Strategy Verification Layer As Market Entry

This note captures a conversation thread between Victor Vorski and Vitali on Integral GTM strategy.

Victor proposed 10 synthesis designs and asked about going to market at scale.

Vitali provided a critical correction: communities as customers is premature.

This note documents the refined market entry model.

Insight: Communities as customers is difficult to imagine now.

However, communities will collectively provide services to the external world until all resources are captured by communities.

Some conversations about Integral reflect this viewpoint.

The market entry should use this dynamic rather than assume community direct purchases.

Model type is the verification layer as the business.

The UNTP/DPP regulatory wave in the EU creates a near-term revenue opportunity.

Hundreds of thousands of companies will need DPPs under EUDR, CSRD, CBAM.

Most will produce unverifiable greenwashing.

A zero-marginal-cost verification service can undercut incumbents and expose fraudulent claims.

Reputation and regulatory clearance become the key differentiators.

This can fund the first Integral-compliant demonstration communities.

Reason 1 is regulatory mandate.
EU has mandated Digital Product Passports.
Companies must comply.
Someone must verify compliance.

Reason 2 is greenwashing problem.
Most DPPs will be false claims.
No third-party verification infrastructure exists.
Market gap for integrity verification.

Reason 3 is near-zero marginal cost.
Software-based verification scales cheaply.
x402 enables micro-payments per verification.
Automated checks plus human audit.

Reason 4 is reputation flywheel.
Verified claims get integrity scores.
Greenwashers exposed publicly.
Integrity standard becomes the trusted brand.

Step 1 is launch verification API.
Use x402 for micro-payments.
Use UNTP format for DPPs.

Step 2 is companies submit DPPs.
Companies seeking integrity certification.
Volume from regulatory compliance needs.

Step 3 is automated checks.
Plus human audit for suspicious claims.
ZK proofs for tamper-evident verification.

Step 4 is integrity scoring.
Verified DPPs get integrity score.
ZK-proof verifiable by anyone.
Scores linkable to public record.

Step 5 is greenwasher exposure.
False claims publicly linkable.
Incentive to get real verification.
Market forces favor integrity.

Step 6 is revenue funding.
Fees from verification services.
Revenue funds next community.
Profit converts to community infrastructure.

Step 7 is community labor.
Communities provide verification labor.
Members earn through verification work.
Skills transfer and capacity building.

Step 8 is flywheel acceleration.
More integrity attracts more volume.
More volume funds more communities.
Network effects compound.

The verification service IS the Interface Cooperative.

It provides services to market entities for monetary payment.

Internally operates through Integral governance.

Revenue converts to tools, machines, land, infrastructure.

Assets absorbed into commons governance.

This is the actual mechanism for resource conversion.

Factor 1 is regulatory wave.
UNTP is known EU mandate.
Companies need verification by 2026-2027.

Factor 2 is clear revenue model.
Per-verification fees.
Freemium tier for market penetration.
Volume scales with regulation.

Factor 3 is no community build-out required.
Service can start immediately.
Team needs engineers and compliance auditors.
Community comes after revenue.

Factor 4 is defensible position.
Trust aggregator is won by first movers.
Reputation compounds over time.
Hard for incumbents to copy quickly.

Market size is millions of EU companies needing DPPs.

Early mover advantage is significant.

Trust aggregator position is won by whoever gets reputation first.

Competition is Big4 doing expensive verification.

Alternative is Integrity DAO  -  cheap, open, ZK-verified.

Next step 1 is draft specific go-to-market for verification service angle.

Next step 2 is identify initial verification metrics and integrity scoring criteria.

Next step 3 is build MVP verification API with x402 and UNTP standards.

Next step 4 is approach first pilot companies for DPP verification.

Next step 5 is use revenue to fund first Integral-compliant demonstration community.
