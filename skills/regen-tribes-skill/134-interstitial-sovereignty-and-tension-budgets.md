# Interstitial Sovereignty And Tension Budgets

The idea: Intentional communities do not need to confront dominant systems to scale.
They can operate in the gaps.
Building functional parallel institutions (education, healthcare, exchange, governance) that become attractive alternatives before any systemic confrontation.

This is not retreat. It is building without asking permission, then demonstrating viability at scale. When the cracks appear in conventional systems (and they will), the infrastructure exists.

Why this is novel: Most scaling discourse focuses on how to spread the idea.
Interstitial sovereignty says: spread the function first, the idea later.
The community does not need the world to believe.
It needs the world to see.

Every intentional community has a tension budget. The amount of internal disagreement it can absorb before fracture.

Different tensions have different budgets:

Ideological purity versus pragmatic adaptation. High budget, but drains when breached.

Governance speed versus inclusivity. Finite budget, depletes with size.

Economic equality versus performance incentives. Constant low-grade drain.

Growth versus intimacy. Often the killer.

Communities do not die from problems. They die from running out of budget for the specific tensions their growth triggers.

This is measurable. Track the tensions active in your community. Monitor the budget. Predict fracture points before they become crises.
