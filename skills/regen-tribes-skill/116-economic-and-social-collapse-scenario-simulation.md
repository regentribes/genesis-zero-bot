# Economic And Social Collapse Scenario Simulation

Four collapse pathways mapped with action strategies for regenerative communities.

Trigger: Strait of Hormuz closure plus private credit bubble burst plus AI data center bubble pop. Converging simultaneously.

Timeline:

Months 1-3: Supply chain shocks. JIT inventory collapses. Grocery stores empty in 72 hours. Gas plus 200-400%. Markets freeze. Private credit defaults cascade.

Months 4-8: Food rationing. National Guard deployed. Draft legislation passes quickly. Digital dollar launched. Emergency digital currency deployed to maintain transaction velocity.

Months 9-18: Second wave. AI data centers shutdown. Server farms go dark. Tech unemployment spikes. Real estate market crashes. Commercial real estate defaults spread to regional banks.

Second year: Structural unemployment at 25-35%. Housing crash. Foreclosure crisis. Community resilience networks activate. Local food production becomes primary survival strategy.

Regen community actions: Activate food production immediately. Pool resources with neighbors. Establish barter networks. Maintain digital communication infrastructure. Build local skill redundancy.

Trigger: Three breadbasket regions fail simultaneously due to drought, flood, and heat. Global grain production drops 40%. Food prices spike 300-500%. Water conflicts escalate.

Timeline:

Year 1: Food price inflation. Riots in urban centers. Government imposes export controls. Water rationing begins in major cities. Climate refugees move in mass numbers toward cooler regions.

Year 2: Water wars intensify. Interstate water agreements collapse. Agricultural regions abandon crops for water-intensive livestock. Food scarcity drives migration patterns.

Year 3: Social fabric tears. Communities reorganize around water and food access. Local governance becomes primary. Central governments retain nominal authority but lose functional control.

Regen community actions: Secure water rights and storage. Establish food sovereignty. Build cooling infrastructure. Develop local seed preservation. Create heat-resistant shelter designs.

Regen community actions: Solar with battery backup is non-negotiable. Offline communication systems. Local water purification. Food preservation skills. Medical training. Seed storage.

Trigger: AI systems automate 40% of jobs in 18 months. Unemployment spikes to 30%. Universal basic income proposals fail politically. Social safety nets collapse under demand. Civil unrest spreads.

Timeline:

Months 1-6: Tech sector layoffs accelerate. Service sector workers displaced. White collar professions begin automating. Professional credential value collapses. Degree ROI becomes negative for most fields.

Year 1: Political radicalization. Two factions emerge. One demands AI prohibition. One demands radical wealth redistribution. Neither consensus reachable. Polarization intensifies.

Year 2: Social services overwhelmed. Food banks lines grow. Homelessness spikes. Property crime increases. Private security industry expands. Wealthy retreat to fortified communities.

Year 3: Parallel economies emerge. People create informal economies outside AI control. Barter networks. Local currencies. Community-based mutual aid. Underground economies grow.

Regen community actions: Develop skills AI cannot replicate. Build local economies. Maintain food sovereignty. Create offline social fabric. Establish local governance. Preserve craftsmanship.

Independence: One week of food storage minimum. Two weeks recommended. Water purification capability. Medical training. Solar charging. Cash reserves. Physical skills.

Community: Skill redundancy. Multiple people with each skill. Shared tools. Community garden. Barter network. Local communication system. Mutual aid agreements.

Long-term: Land access. Water rights. Seed preservation. Tool maintenance capability. Building skills. Medical skills. Teaching skills. Animal husbandry.

Infrastructure: Local power generation. Water collection. Food production. Communication systems. Health response capability. Seed bank. Tool library.

Knowledge: Survival skills documented. Local history preserved. Agricultural knowledge maintained. Building maintenance capability. Medical training.

Governance: Local decision making. Resource allocation systems. Conflict resolution. Communication protocols. Emergency response plans.

During collapse, regenerative communities form the backbone of survival networks. They maintain food production. They share resources. They preserve knowledge. They organize mutual aid.

The network effect: Connected communities share resources across distance. Food surplus moves to deficit areas. Tools shared across regions. Skills transferred between groups. Information propagates through trusted channels.

Activation protocol: When external systems fail, internal systems activate. Communities shift from development mode to survival mode. Leadership decentralizes. Resource allocation becomes local. Communication maintains network cohesion.

The goal is not to survive alone. The goal is to maintain the network that keeps everyone alive.
