# The Organ Social Domain

Organ (metaphor for a functional subsystem or team):

An organ is one neighborhood.
Twelve to 200 people.

Governance at this scale needs structure.
Not hierarchy. Structure.

The council holds the decisions.
Tissue (metaphor for a connected group or network):

Tissue (metaphor for connected group or network): Each tissue sends one representative.
The council meets once per week.

The council handles four things.
Infrastructure. Disputes. Planning. Resources.

Infrastructure decisions answer: what to build. How to maintain. Who pays.

Disputes means conflicts between tissues.
The council mediates. It does not judge.

Planning means the council sets the quarterly direction.
The tissues execute.

Resources means the council allocates shared tools and land and water and energy.

Consent still rules.
A decision passes when no council member blocks it.

Each council member can block on behalf of their tissue.
This is their job. Blocking is serious.

The council has a guide. Not a chair.
The guide holds the process.

Roles at the organ level: infrastructure lead. Food lead. Energy lead. Education lead.

Each role has a charter.
Purpose defines why. Scope defines what. Authority defines decisions. Accountability defines review.

The council reviews each role every quarter.
Accountability means show your work. Explain your decisions. Accept feedback.

Land is held in common.
No one person owns the land. The organ holds the title.

Buildings are owned by the builders. Or by the organ.
This is decided case by case.

When someone leaves their building can be bought by the organ. Or removed. Or passed to family.
No fixed rule. Case by case.

What matters: the land stays in the commons. The knowledge stays in the commons.

The commons is protected by the council.
The council is guided by consent.

Conflict at the organ level: two tissues disagree. The council mediates.
If mediation fails an external mediator helps. They do not decide. They help.

Communication: each tissue posts a weekly update. What they did. What they will do.

Decision logs are public. Anyone can read them.
This prevents power concentration. Hidden decisions enable capture.

Open records are the anti-capture mechanism.
