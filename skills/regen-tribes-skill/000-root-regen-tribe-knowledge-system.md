Accelerate the development of Regenerative Neighborhoods.
Accelerate the development of community infrastructure.

The mission excludes hierarchy.
The mission excludes rent.

Every system fits one of five levels.

Cell (metaphor for a basic unit or module):

Cell (metaphor for basic unit or module): Level 1 is the Cell.
One person.
One home.
One skill.
The smallest unit that can act.

Tissue (metaphor for a connected group or network):

Tissue (metaphor for connected group or network): Level 2 is the Tissue.
Three to twelve people.
A household or a core team.
Trust forms here.
Small groups share and decide.

Organ (metaphor for a functional subsystem or team):

Level 3 is the Organ.
Twelve to 200 people.
One neighborhood.
Shared infrastructure.
Local governance.
The scale where collective action works.

Level 4 is the System.
A bioregion.
Multiple neighborhoods.
Trade. Federation.
Distances that need transport or communication.

Body (metaphor for the complete collective or organism):

Level 5 is the Body.
The whole planetary network.
Knowledge commons.
Standards that let bioregions talk.
Shared infrastructure at global scale.

The five levels give every system a size and scope.

The four domains give every system a type.

The five levels and four domains form a grid.

The loop principle governs physical infrastructure.
Output of one system becomes input of another.
Water loops. Food loops. Energy loops. Waste loops.

The trust principle governs social infrastructure.
Trust grows through repeated interaction.
Trust follows evidence over claims.

The memory principle governs information infrastructure.
Knowledge persists across time.
Knowledge connects across space.

The circulation principle governs exchange infrastructure.
Value circulates.
Value does not accumulate.
Value does not extract.

The mission requires four pillars.
Physical independence. Economic independence.
Governance independence. Social independence.

Physical independence means closed-loop systems.
Energy from sun. Water from rain. Food from soil.
Waste returns to soil. No external dependency.

Economic independence means no debt.
No rent. No interest.
Time banking. Gift economy.
Contribution measured. Extraction visible.

Governance independence means consent-based decision making.
No external authority. No hierarchy.
Roles guide. Roles do not command.

Social independence means high-trust networks.
Trust emerges from shared experience.
Trust deepens through shared risk.
Trust solidifies through shared creation.

Every system must resist four types of capture.

Capital capture means wealth leaving the community.
Prevention: circulation over compounding.
Surplus flows back in.
The commons retains surplus.

Narrative capture means attention serving outside interests.
Prevention: commons over platform.
Shared story stays local.
Shared story serves local purpose.

Identity capture means people sorted into scores.
Prevention: contribution over score.
What you do matters.
Who you know does not matter.

Governance capture means rules set by outsiders.
Prevention: exit-friendly design.
Leaving must be easy.
No lock-in.
