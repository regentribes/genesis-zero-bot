# Community Alchemy Regenerative Neighborhood Playbook

This note captures the Community Alchemy Playbook framework for regenerative neighborhood creation.

The playbook has 11 stages. Each stage has a focus. Each focus builds the neighborhood.

The stages are sequential. Each stage prepares for the next. Skipping stages creates gaps.

Vision is the foundation. Vision answers why. Why does this community exist. Why does it matter.

Vision guides all decisions. Decisions serve vision. Decisions that contradict vision are wrong.

Vision requires clarity. Clarity requires dialogue. Dialogue requires time.

Members make the community. Members bring skills. Members bring resources. Members bring relationships.

Recruitment follows vision. Vision attracts members. Members who resonate with vision stay.

Recruitment requires selectivity. Selectivity requires criteria. Criteria emerge from vision.

Governance is how decisions happen. Governance determines power. Power determines behavior.

Tissue (metaphor for a connected group or network):

Tissue (metaphor for connected group or network): The tissue uses consent. Consent means no objection. Objection requires resolution.

Governance structures include sociocracy. Sociocracy uses circles. Circles double-link roles.

Governance structures include holacracy. Holacracy uses roles. Roles are explicit.

Infrastructure enables life. Infrastructure includes water. Infrastructure includes energy. Infrastructure includes shelter.

Business models fund infrastructure. Business models generate revenue. Revenue sustains operations.

Models include time banking. Time banking uses hours. Hours are equal exchange.

Models include cooperative ownership. Ownership is shared. Shared ownership distributes power.

Land is the base. Land holds infrastructure. Land holds community.

Acquisition requires legal form. Legal forms include lease. Lease provides access without ownership.

Acquisition requires location analysis. Analysis includes water. Analysis includes sun. Analysis includes community proximity.

Funding enables construction. Funding comes from grants. Funding comes from loans. Funding comes from members.

Grant funding requires applications. Applications require alignment. Alignment with funder values.

Member funding requires trust. Trust requires track record. Track record requires demonstration.

Marketing attracts members. Marketing builds relationships. Relationships enable federation.

Marketing communicates vision. Vision attracts resonance. Resonance enables recruitment.

Marketing uses stories. Stories convey experience. Experience builds confidence.

Sustainability is the goal. Sustainability closes loops. Loops eliminate waste.

System (metaphor for an integrated whole or network):
Systems include water. Rainwater catchment. Filtration. Reuse.

Systems include energy. Solar generation. Storage. Distribution.

Systems include food. Permaculture design. Vertical growing. Aquaponics.

Systems include waste. Composting. Biogas. Nutrient recovery.

Construction implements the plan. Construction requires skills. Skills require training.

Construction follows permaculture principles. Principles guide placement. Placement improves flow.

Construction uses appropriate technology. Technology matches skills. Skills match resources.

Culture is how life happens. Culture emerges from interaction. Interaction builds relationships.

Culture requires rituals. Rituals mark transitions. Transitions create meaning.

Culture requires celebration. Celebration creates positive memory. Memory builds identity.

Culture requires conflict resolution. Resolution maintains trust. Trust enables cooperation.

Management maintains function. Review improves function. Improvement is continuous.

Management uses metrics. Metrics measure outcomes. Outcomes guide adjustment.

Review uses feedback. Feedback comes from members. Feedback comes from systems.

Dunbar number is 150. This is the cognitive limit of social relationships.

Community size affects intimacy. Smaller communities have higher intimacy. Larger communities have more resources.

Upper limit is 250 people. Lower limit is 3 families. The right size serves the vision.

Stage 1 builds vision. Stage 2 builds team. Stage 3 builds governance.

Stage 4 builds economics. Stage 5 builds land base. Stage 6 builds funding.

Stage 7 builds presence. Stage 8 builds systems. Stage 9 builds physical.

Stage 10 builds culture. Stage 11 builds longevity.

The sequence is not rigid. Stages overlap. Stages repeat.

The goal is tissue formation. Tissue serves bioregion. Bioregion serves planet.
