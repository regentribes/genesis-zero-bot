# Civilizational Transition Scenarios

Scenario 1 is Managed Transition.
Proactive reorganization via policy, community building, trust networks.
Population stays stable with redistribution.
Timeline is 20 to 50 years.

Scenario 2 is Disorderly Decline.
Economic and environmental degradation without coordination.
Population decreases 15 to 30 percent over 30 years.
Timeline is 15 to 40 years.

Scenario 3 is Polycrisis Cascade.
Multiple systemic failures: climate plus economy plus geopolitics.
Population decreases 40 to 60 percent.
Timeline is 10 to 25 years.

Scenario 4 is Rapid Collapse.
Tipping points trigger fast systemic breakdown.
Population decreases 70 to 90 percent.
Timeline is 3 to 10 years.

Precedent 1 is Bronze Age collapse.
Type is Localized.
Population loss is 50 percent in Eastern Mediterranean.
Recovery takes 300 to 500 years.

Precedent 2 is Roman collapse.
Type is Gradual.
Population loss is 30 percent in Western Europe.
Recovery takes 500 or more years.

Precedent 3 is Maya collapse.
Type is Environmental.
Population loss is 90 percent in core region.
Recovery never happened.

Precedent 4 is Irish Potato Famine.
Type is Managed versus unmanaged.
Population loss is 25 percent.
Recovery takes 2 generations.

Precedent 5 is Soviet dissolution.
Type is Managed.
Population loss is less than 5 percent.
Recovery takes 10 years.

The critical window is between cultural collapse and material collapse.
Cultural collapse is stage 3.
Material collapse is stage 4.

High-trust societies can do managed reorganization.
Low-trust equals violent reorganization.

The implication: Trust infrastructure now determines which scenario plays out.
Communities building trust networks are building collapse resistance.
