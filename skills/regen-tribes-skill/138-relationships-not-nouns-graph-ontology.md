# Relationships Not Nouns Graph Ontology

Relationships. Not things. Not nouns. Connections.

The rock, plant, animal, man. They are not separate entities evolving upward. They are relationships becoming more aware of themselves.

Ibn Arabi knew what the graph knows: nothing exists in isolation. Every node is defined by its edges.

The self is a relationship. Observer observes observed.

Consciousness is a relationship. Subject meets object.

Language is a relationship. Word meets meaning.

Community is a relationship. Me meets you.

Maybe even: Intelligence is a relationship. Pattern meets pattern.

The practical implication: When you see relationships, you see the structure beneath the surface. The ecotypes. The edges where novelty lives. The feedback loops that become stories.

Most knowledge systems treat things as primary. Entities, nouns, objects. Relationships are secondary properties of things.

Graph ontology inverts this. Relationships are primary. Things are patterns of relationships.

For regenerative communities: the network is the community. The edges are what matter. The nodes are where edges meet.
