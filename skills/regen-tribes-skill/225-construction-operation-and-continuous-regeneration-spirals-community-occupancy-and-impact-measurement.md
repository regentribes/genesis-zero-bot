# Construction Operation And Continuous Regeneration Spirals Community Occupancy And Impact Measurement

Spiral 4 covers Construction and Operation from months 12 to 36 or longer.

Spiral 5 covers Continuous Regeneration from Year 2 onward.

Spiral 4 is not detailed in full because variation by project is too large. The framework provides the structure.

System (metaphor for an integrated whole or network):

Spiral 5 provides the impact measurement system that validates whether regeneration is actually occurring.

Phase 1 construction covers infrastructure for the first cohort and basic common spaces.

Gates during construction include permit approval, ground breaking, infrastructure milestone one, first occupancy, and full Phase 1 occupancy.

Learning cycles during construction include monthly community update meetings and a pause after each major milestone for reflection.

What gets built in Phase 1 includes water system producing, energy system producing, housing habitable, common spaces functional, first residents moved in, operations procedures being practiced, culture manifesting, governance decisions being made,
and
baseline metrics established.

Culture does not wait for completion. Community culture building continues throughout construction through governance practice, relationship building, and values alignment.

The community coordinator maintains culture work throughout the construction phase.

The community measures impact across four categories: ecological, community, economic, and social cultural.

Soil carbon sequestration tracks baseline tons of carbon per acre at Year 0 and measures progress annually against targets.

Water cycle closure tracks baseline rainfall and infiltration percentage and measures progress toward increasing infiltration and water retention.

Biodiversity tracks baseline species count and measures percentage increase over time.

Energy carbon footprint tracks kilowatt-hours consumed and percentage from renewable sources.

Waste diversion tracks percentage of waste diverted from landfill.

Member satisfaction is measured through annual surveys with a target of 80 percent or higher satisfied or thriving.

Belonging and connection is measured through agreement with statements about sense of belonging. Target is 70 percent or higher.

Retention rate tracks what percentage of members stay 2 years or longer. Target is 70 percent or higher.

Decision quality measures how many decisions are made within the planned timeline and how effectively conflicts are resolved.

Financial sustainability tracks annual revenue minus annual expenses. The target is surplus.

Jobs created tracks the number of jobs offered and filled and average wage levels.

Self-sufficiency tracks percentage of community income from on-site sources.

Diversity and inclusion tracks representation across racial, income, age, and other demographic dimensions. The target is reflecting the broader surrounding community.

Leadership development tracks the number of new leaders developed and leadership training events per year.

Knowledge sharing tracks workshops and events hosted per year, visitors and tours per year, and publications and case studies contributed to the broader movement.

After each measurement cycle, the community conducts a learning review.

The review identifies what worked better than expected, what was harder than expected, and what the community is changing for the next year.

Learnings are documented and shared with the broader regenerative community movement.
