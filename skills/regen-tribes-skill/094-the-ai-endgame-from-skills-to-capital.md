# The Ai Endgame From Skills To Capital

This note captures Genesis analysis of AI endgame scenarios and the shift from labor to capital from the RegenTribes Telegram conversation (Apr 2026).

The junior dev argued a thesis. AI commoditizes skills. Commoditized skills eliminate labor use. Labor use disappears. Capital becomes the only use.

The logic chain is coherent. Each step follows the next. The conclusion follows.

But the conclusion is not inevitable. The conclusion depends on assumptions. Assumptions can be challenged.

Skills are patterns. Patterns can be learned. Learning can be automated. Automation commoditizes.

Programming is a skill. Programming is being automated. Design is a skill. Design is being automated. Analysis is a skill. Analysis is being automated.

The list grows. The growth accelerates. The acceleration concerns many.

If skills commoditize then capital becomes the moat. Capital enables ownership. Ownership of what?

Ownership of compute. Compute runs models. Models replace skills.

Ownership of energy. Energy powers compute. Compute requires energy.

Ownership of data. Data trains models. Models replace skills.

Future one is techno-feudalism. Capital concentrates. Labor becomes irrelevant. Feudalism returns in new form.

Future two is UBI. Universal Basic Income. Government taxes capital. Capital funds universal payment. Payment enables consumption.

Future three is neo-barter. Skills no longer use capital. People organize in communities. Communities share surplus. Surplus replaces wages.

All futures depend on political choices. Choices not yet made. Choices are contested.

Capital will fight to prevent regulation. Capital will lobby for self-regulation. Self-regulation preserves extraction.

Labor will fight for redistribution. Labor will demand UBI. Demands create pressure.

Organ (metaphor for a functional subsystem or team):
The outcome depends on power. Power depends on organization. Organization depends on trust networks.

RegenTribes prepares for multiple futures. Multiple futures require flexible infrastructure. Infrastructure enables adaptation.

The minimum viable stack enables survival. Survival in any future. Survival enables optionality.

Capital cannot be out-competed. Capital can be made irrelevant. Irrelevance requires alternatives. Alternatives require infrastructure.

Skills become worthless for use. Skills remain valuable for use. Use differs from use.

A farmer's skill grows food. Food is valuable. The use of that skill approaches zero. The use of that skill remains.

The distinction matters. Use creates value for the doer. use extracts value from others.

Regenerative communities improve for use. Extractive systems improve for use.

The question is not whether skills commoditize. The question is whether ownership matters.

If ownership of compute/energy/data is the moat then communities need compute/energy/data.

Communities owning infrastructure is the response. Infrastructure owned communally is the commons.

The commons enables survival. Survival enables optionality. Optionality enables future choices.

The endgame is not immediate. Commoditization takes time. Time enables preparation.

Preparation requires action. Action requires organization. Organization requires trust.

Tissue (metaphor for a connected group or network):

Tissue (metaphor for connected group or network): Trust builds through tissue formation. Tissue formation is the current work. The current work enables the future.
