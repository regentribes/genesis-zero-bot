# The Group Physical Domain

Three people with shared tools build more than ten people with no tools.

Tissue (metaphor for a connected group or network):

Tissue (metaphor for connected group or network): A shared workshop is the first physical infrastructure of a tissue.
The workshop holds a welder. It holds a drill press.
It holds an angle grinder. It holds a 3D printer. It holds hand tools.

Capitalism prices this at what a skilled person earns in one to four months of full-time work.
A community builds it for the effort of one season of shared labor.
Buy used. Fix it yourself.
Learn repair as you go.

What one person fabricates in a week of precision work. A mill for water systems.
What one person fabricates in a week of precision work. It makes parts for sensors.

The tissue workshop produces sensor mounts.
It produces pipe fittings. It produces enclosures. It produces replacement parts.

Production for members works like this.
The tissue makes things for its members.
Members contribute time credits.

Organ (metaphor for a functional subsystem or team):

The tissue makes things for the organ.
The organ pays in goods or labor.

No money enters the picture at this level.
Time and goods only.
Money introduces extraction.

System (metaphor for an integrated whole or network):

Maintenance is part of the system.
Machines break. Someone learns to fix each machine.
This person becomes the maintenance lead.

Tool library model works like this.
The tissue owns tools. Members borrow them. Members return them clean.
This prevents the common failure.
Each household buys What one skilled person makes in a week of focused effort. Each uses it once.

Training goes in steps.
Operator. Builder. Designer. Teacher.
Certification means you can teach the skill to someone else.
This is the only certification that matters. Not paper. Demonstrated skill.

Safety is the first lesson.
Every machine can hurt you.
First aid kit is mandatory.
Fire extinguisher is mandatory.
Eye wash is mandatory.

The tissue maintains the land it sits on.
Fence. Access road. Water. Power.
These tasks go into the time banking system.

The tissue physical layer feeds the organ physical layer.
The organ needs what the tissue makes.
The tissue needs what the organ provides.
This is the beginning of the exchange network.
