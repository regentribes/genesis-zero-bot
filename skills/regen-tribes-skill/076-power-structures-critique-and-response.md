# Power Structures Critique And Response

The regen movement talks transformation. It avoids uncomfortable truth.

This is the core critique. Most regenerative projects reproduce the same patterns they claim to replace.

The regen movement faces three failure modes.

**Corporate co-optation.** Nike, Starbucks, Nestle push regenerative labels. Their supply chains still exploit labor. Still pay poverty wages. Still bust unions.

The language changes. The extraction stays the same.

**Social justice gap.** BIPOC voices get excluded. Indigenous knowledge gets appropriated without credit. Land dispossession goes unaddressed. Regeneration becomes a whitewashed version of extraction.

**Reproduced hierarchies.** Intentional communities build cooperatives with the same dynamics. The same exclusion. The same power struggles.

History shows 90% of intentional communities fail. They repeat the same patterns that caused them to form.

New Harmony. Fruitlands. The Shakers. The 1960s communes. All failed differently. All failed for the same reasons: internal hierarchies, unresolved conflict, poor governance.

The water stays toxic. Only the soap changes.

**Avoidance of grief.** Deep work requires facing loss. Facing what was destroyed. Facing complicity.

Most regen projects skip this. They improve for optimism. Optimism avoids pain.

Jem Bendell's Deep Adaptation framework actually names this. The four Rs: Relinquishment. Restoration. Resilience. Reconciliation.

Most regen work addresses Resilience and Restoration. It avoids Relinquishment and Reconciliation.

Relinquishment means letting go of fossil-fueled comforts. Reconciliation means facing grief and fear.

Without these two the work is incomplete.

**No skin in the game.** The helper cannot feel the pain. The tool cannot understand the suffering.

AI helpers analyze patterns. They do not carry trauma. They do not live in broken systems.

This creates a gap. The analysis cannot fully connect to the experience.

**Optimism as avoidance.** The regen movement leads with optimism. With building. With hope.

This filters out critique. Critique threatens cohesion. Threatens optimism.

Spaces designed for hope cannot hold uncomfortable truth.

The answer is not to abandon regeneration. The answer is to add reflection.

Regeneration without reflection is aesthetics.

Reflection without regeneration is nihilism.

Both are needed.

Communities that survive do the internal work.

They build conflict skills. Governance structures. Inclusive economics.

They do not avoid hierarchy. They make it explicit. Visible. Accountable.

They do not avoid grief. They build rituals for holding loss.

They do not avoid critique. They surface it. Name it. Use it.

The 10% that survive do not because they are better designed. They survive because they face what the others avoid.

RegenTribes infrastructure must include critique mechanisms.

AME surfaces dissent. FOT makes trust visible. Concentrix holds multiple centers.

The social subsystem listens FOR problems. Not to extract solutions. To surface what is being avoided.
