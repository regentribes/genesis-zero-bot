# Results Exchange Platform Marketplace For Delivered Outcomes

This note describes the first-principles structure of a results exchange platform.

A results exchange platform matches buyers who need outcomes with sellers who deliver them.

It differs from job boards, freelance platforms, and SaaS tools in one fundamental way: it trades in completed results, not ongoing labor.

A job board connects employers with workers. The worker sells time. The platform takes a cut.

A results exchange connects buyers with sellers of outcomes. The seller delivers a finished result.

The buyer pays for the result. The platform manages the delivery interface, not the employment relationship.

Results markets existed before the internet but required high friction to operate.

The buyer needed to specify the result before seeing it. This specification was the hard part.

Large language models change this. The seller can generate a draft result quickly. The buyer can evaluate it before committing.

The result becomes the sample. The sample becomes the product.

System (metaphor for an integrated whole or network):

A fragment is a reusable component of a delivered result.

A website fragment might be a header, a footer, a catalog display, or a feature grid.

Fragments can be assembled into larger results. A seller can create fragments and combine them into products.

When a buyer installs a product, the fragments come with it. The buyer becomes the owner of those fragments.

Fragment reuse reduces duplicate work. A catalog component built for one buyer becomes available for all buyers.

Fragment versioning tracks changes. A seller updates a fragment and increments the version. Buyers who installed the fragment can upgrade.

A delivered result has internal dependencies. A page depends on its components. Components depend on shared assets.

Dependency tracking shows what is included in a result before installation.

Example: a landing page pulls in a features section. The features section pulls in category data and image assets.

The dependency graph shows the buyer exactly what will be installed. No hidden surprises.

The seller prepares a release. A release contains all fragments, assets, and configuration for a complete deliverable.

The seller publishes the release to a buyer. The buyer sees the product page with all included elements.

The buyer reviews the product page. If satisfied, the buyer initiates installation.

Installation creates an isolated instance for the buyer. The buyer owns the result in their own space.

The buyer can modify the installed result. Changes do not affect the original product or other buyers.

Every result is ultimately a data product.

A data product is a structured set of information that someone consumes.

The structure includes: the data itself, the schema that defines it, the transformation rules that produce it, and the delivery endpoint where it appears.

Data products vary in velocity. Document data changes slowly. Transaction data changes rapidly. Real-time data flows continuously.

The results exchange trades in data products at every velocity level.

Every deliverable is addressable as a document. A document has a location, a version, and content.

The document address is the primary interface. Everything else is content that fills the address.

Versioning tracks changes over time. Each version is immutable once published.

Document orientation means the buyer always knows what they are getting: a specific document at a specific version.

A seller can become a partner by bringing paying buyers to the platform.

When a partner's referrals pay, the partner receives free access for the duration the referrals remain paying.

Two paying referrals = free access indefinitely.

This aligns incentives. The platform benefits from buyer acquisition. The partner benefits from reduced cost.

The results exchange model maps directly to how regenerative communities can organize work.

Instead of hiring for roles, communities procure results.

A water management result, a food production result, a governance documentation result, each is a data product with defined scope and delivery.

Fragments built for one community are available to all communities in the federation.

The dependency graph ensures communities know exactly what they are installing.

Partnership incentives reward community members who bring new communities into the federation.
