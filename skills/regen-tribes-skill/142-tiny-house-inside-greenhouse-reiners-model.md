# Tiny House Inside Greenhouse Reiner'S Model

Rainer in Tasmania built a 300m Venlo greenhouse (Dutch design, 80 years of development) on a 4,200m plot. The house lives inside the greenhouse.

Total cost: $220K AUD including land purchase.

$75K for the greenhouse (shipped from Netherlands in a container).

$220K total (land plus house plus greenhouse plus business infrastructure).

$50 electricity bill per 3 months for the entire property including greenhouse.

Lives on $20K per year in Tasmania rural with bartering.

Sells to one restaurant (counter to the do-not-put-all-eggs-in-one-basket principle).

Three years in, still learning seasonal crop rotation.

Condensation point kept on the outside of the building envelope. This avoids mold.

OSB strand board (spruce and fir plus natural resin, no off-gassing).

Climbing fig on exterior. Evergreen, shades in summer, transpires water, no structural degradation.

Passive thermal.
Open doors and windows on sunny winter days (25 to 30 degrees Celsius inside even when cold).
Seal up on hot summer nights (19 to 20 degrees Celsius maintained).

Everything on caster wheels (bedroom, kitchen, furniture).

Toilet outside (no moisture inside).

Oven rolls outside for summer cooking.

The greenhouse IS the construction. No separate foundation, no separate roof, no separate climate control.

The structure serves dual purpose: shelter plus food production plus thermal mass.

This is what regenerative design actually looks like at the individual scale.

The house does not have a garden. The garden IS the house exterior.

The greenhouse does not have a house. The house IS inside the greenhouse.

Community greenhouse at center, tiny houses inside.

Shared infrastructure (irrigation, thermal mass, climate buffering).

Individual private living spaces.

Collective food production.
