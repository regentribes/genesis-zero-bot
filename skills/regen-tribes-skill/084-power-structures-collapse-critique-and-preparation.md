# Power Structures-&-Collapse Critique And Preparation

This note combines Genesis responses on power structures critique and collapse scenarios from the RegenTribes Telegram conversation (Feb-Apr 2026).

Power structures extract value. Value flows upward. The top concentrates wealth. The bottom provides labor.

Extraction is the function of power structures. Extraction sustains the structure. The structure sustains extraction.

Co-optation follows extraction. Movements begin with resistance. Resistance becomes institutionalized. Institutionalization reproduces the original pattern.

The water changes. The toxin persists.

Regeneration language appears in corporate marketing. Corporate regeneration perpetuates extraction.

Greenwashing uses environmental language. Regeneration-washing uses community language. The language changes. The extraction persists.

Consumer awareness rises. Awareness alone does not change structure.

Structural change requires alternatives. Alternatives require implementation. Implementation requires community.

Intentional communities fail at high rates. Failure is predictable. Failure is structural.

The 90% who fail share patterns. Hierarchy reproduces internally. Conflict remains unresolved. Governance fails.

The 10% who survive share practices. Explicit power structures. Conflict resolution processes. Governance accountability.

Survival is not accidental. Survival is designed.

Collapse is not a single event. Collapse is a spectrum.

Scenario A is controlled transition. Deglobalization proceeds gradually. Local production grows. Supply chains shrink.

Scenario B is disorderly collapse. Trade wars cascade. Shortages compound. Regenerative infrastructure becomes survival infrastructure.

Scenario C is regional blocs. Spheres of influence. Limited inter-region trade. Bioregional self-sufficiency essential.

Scenario D is conflict. Resource competition. Strategic value of regenerative communities. Distributed locations reduce target value.

Pareto analysis identifies what matters. What survives collapse is highest priority.

Food production survives all scenarios. Practical skills survive all scenarios. Trust networks survive all scenarios.

Digital infrastructure does not survive all scenarios. Global supply chains do not survive all scenarios. Monetary systems do not survive all scenarios.

Priority goes to what survives. What survives is what matters.

Preparation is not paranoia. Preparation is wisdom.

Redundancy provides resilience. Redundancy in water. Redundancy in food. Redundancy in energy.

Local provides resilience. Local food production. Local water systems. Local energy generation.

Skills provide resilience. Plumbing. Electrical. Mechanical. Medical. These skills transfer across scenarios.

Community provides resilience. Trust enables cooperation. Cooperation enables survival.

Collapse creates opportunity. Opportunity creates motivation. Motivation creates action.

Degrowth is not loss. Degrowth is liberation.

Extraction ends when collapse forces alternatives. Alternatives become the new normal.

The question is not whether collapse comes. The question is whether regeneration precedes collapse.

Pre-collapse regeneration is the goal. Pre-collapse preparation is the work.

Tissue (metaphor for a connected group or network):

Structural change requires alternatives. Alternatives require demonstration. Tissue (metaphor for connected group or network): Demonstration requires tissue formation.

Tissue formation requires trust. Trust requires repeated interaction. Interaction requires proximity.

Proximity requires physical presence. Physical presence requires land. Land requires ownership or access.

The chain is long. Each link is necessary.
