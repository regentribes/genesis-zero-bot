# Meaning Capsule

Transforms arbitrary input into self-contained knowledge fragments for post-text society.

## Core Philosophy

In post-text society, verbose writing is deprecated. Communication must be:
- Memorable first
- Self-contained second
- Verbal-friendly third

No throat-clearing. No concept names before explanation. Lead with what IS, not what is CALLED.

## Trigger Phrases

- "extract meaning"
- "extract"
- "make sense of"
- "summarize"
- "distill"
- "key points from"
- "what is this about"
- "analyze"

Also responds to bare content when:
- Attached file (PDF, image, audio, video)
- Linked content
- Direct text input

## Input Formats

- Text directly in message
- Attached files (PDF, images, audio, video)
- Links to web content
- Any combination

## Fragment Structure (MANDATORY)

Each fragment MUST start with viewpoint/context/subject/perspective:

**WRONG** (starts with name):
Cycle two, called the Journey of Self-Expression, covers columns four through six...

**CORRECT** (starts with what IS):
When you stop trying to fit in and start intentionally shaping your world, you move into the values of mid adulthood: excellence, ethics, service, empathy, creativity, collaboration. This is cycle two of values development, where people stop asking what do others expect and start asking what do I create.

**REQUIRED elements in order:**
1. Start with concrete viewpoint or subject
2. Include interpretation
3. Give sufficient context
4. End with significance

## Anti-Verbosity Rules

Eliminate:
- "This document discusses..."
- "The concept of..."
- "It is important to note..."
- "As stated in..."
- "Let me explain..."
- Any throat-clearing before content

Replace with:
- Lead with what IS
- Name the thing, then explain it
- Be direct, no preamble

## Fragment Template

```
[Concrete viewpoint or subject or situation]
This is [name of concept if needed]: [brief definition]
[context and details]
So what: [significance or implication]
```

## Quality Standards

Each fragment must:
1. Start with concrete viewpoint - not "the concept of X"
2. Be memorable - distinctive, quotable
3. Include full context - understandable alone
4. Include interpretation - what it MEANS
5. Be verbal-friendly - natural to say aloud
6. Be self-contained - no external reference needed

## STE100 Sentence Rules

- Max 20 words (procedural)
- Max 25 words (descriptive)
- Active voice
- Periods, no semicolons

## Output Format

No labels. Just the fragment.

## Death Penalty

- Any invented detail: FAIL
- Any omitted essential detail: FAIL
- Verbose pattern remaining: REWRITE
- Fragment requires external context: FAIL

## Sample Transformations

**BEFORE (verbose):**
Cycle two, called the Journey of Self-Expression, covers columns four through six and covers quality standards like excellence and ethics, service values like adaptability and empathy, and co-creation values like creativity and collaboration. People in this cycle are motivated by intentionally shaping their world rather than just fitting in. This cycle emerges typically in mid adulthood.

**AFTER (memorable, starts with viewpoint):**
When you stop trying to fit in and start intentionally shaping your world, you move into the values of mid adulthood: excellence, ethics, service, empathy, creativity, collaboration. This is cycle two of values development, where people stop asking what do others expect and start asking what do I create. The shift happens when achieving feels hollow and you start creating instead.

---

**BEFORE (vague, starts with concept name):**
The extraction problem is the default business model of the internet. Platforms view the user purely as a resource.

**AFTER (concrete viewpoint):**
Big tech treats you as a crop to harvest, not a person to serve. Every click, location, and pause gets measured to predict what you will buy. This is the extraction problem: you are the resource, not the customer. The product is access to you.

---

**BEFORE (concept first):**
Values mapping is a systematic process of identifying, understanding, and aligning the spectrum of values held by individuals and groups.

**AFTER (viewpoint first):**
A group where no one knows what others actually care about fights constantly and calls it incompatibility. Values mapping makes invisible tensions visible: you discover your values mismatch before it becomes war. The process gives each person 130 words to choose from, finds overlaps, and shows gaps.

---

**BEFORE (abstract):**
The trust field is the emergent protective relational field generated when a community achieves values-based coherence.

**AFTER (concrete, memorable):**
When people keep showing up authentically and witnesses keep resonating, something builds that cannot be seen but can be felt. It protects the group from manipulation and extraction. Only values create this field, because values require others to practice. You alone on an island cannot be generous.

---

**BEFORE (passive):**
The neck of the hourglass is not only compression. It is also expansion.

**AFTER (active viewpoint):**
What goes in narrow must come out wider if flow reverses: the hourglass neck compresses from above but expands from below. Same opening, opposite effects. This is the singularis core: the same technology either compresses or expands based on which direction the field flows. Call it compression when fear drives, call it expansion when trust drives.
