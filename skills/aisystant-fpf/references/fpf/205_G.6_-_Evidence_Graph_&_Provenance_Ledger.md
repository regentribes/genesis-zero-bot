## G.6 - Evidence Graph & Provenance Ledger

**Tag.** Architectural pattern
**Stage.** design‚Äëtime (assembly) + run‚Äëtime (telemetry ingestion)
**Primary output.** A notation‚Äëindependent `EvidenceGraph` + a stable `PathId` / `PathSliceId` citation surface + an SCR projection (‚ÄúAssurance SCR‚Äù) suitable for audit, selection explainability, and refresh/RSCR wiring.
**Primary hooks.** A.10 (evidence anchors/carriers; SCR/RSCR anchoring), B.3 (assurance lanes and `F/G/R` skeleton), F.9 (BridgeCard/CL), G.4 (CAL `EvidenceProfiles` + `ProofLedger` linkage), `G.Core` (Part‚ÄëG invariants, RSCR trigger catalogue, default‚Äëownership index), E.18/A.21 (GateCrossing + CrossingBundle checks), F.17 (UTS publication), F.15 (RSCR), E.10 (LEX), E.5.* (notation‚Äëindependence discipline).
**Non‚Äëduplication note.** Universal Part‚ÄëG invariants (no shadow specs; Bridge‚Äëonly crossings; tri‚Äëstate discipline; penalties‚Üí`R_eff` only; P2W split; typed/id‚Äëbased RSCR causes; single‚Äëowner defaults; Œî‚Äëdiscipline) are owned by `G.Core` and are *cited* via `CC‚ÄëGCORE‚Äë*`. This pattern defines only the *EvidenceGraph kit* and its path‚Äëaddressable provenance surfaces.

### G.6:1 - Problem frame

SoTA claims, operators, and method families are admitted (or gated) using assurance signals derived from diverse artefacts and anchors. FPF already mandates **Evidence Graph Referring** (A.10), lane discipline, and the assurance skeleton (B.3). What is often still missing in practice is a *first‚Äëclass, citable* object that makes the provenance of an admission/decision **addressable**:

* *exactly which* anchors and bindings were used,
* *under which* `ReferencePlane` and `BoundedContext`,
* *with which* explicit crossings and penalty policies,
* *for which* time window (freshness/decay),
* in a way that selectors, audits, and maturity transitions can cite without copying tables or re‚Äëtelling a story.

This pattern introduces the missing kit: a typed, lane‚Äëaware `EvidenceGraph` plus stable `PathId` / `PathSliceId` addresses that downstream LOG, UTS, parity, and refresh can cite.

**Why here (not in G.4)?** G.4 owns CAL artefacts (EvidenceProfiles, ProofLedger, acceptance policies). G.6 packages *cross‚Äëartefact provenance* as a graph and mints *path identities* that downstream surfaces can cite without duplicating CAL tables or re‚Äëinventing legality rules.

### G.6:2 - Problem

1. Readers cannot reliably **audit crossing/penalty and decay impacts** on claims without chasing many tables and informal narratives.
2. Cross‚ÄëContext/plane reuse must remain **Bridge‚Äëonly and explicit**, but provenance often hides crossings (or treats them as ‚Äúobvious‚Äù).
3. Selection and maturity decisions need a stable **path address** to re‚Äëcheck later, including after edition/policy/freshness changes.

### G.6:3 - Forces

| Force                        | Tension                                                                             |
| ---------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Provenance vs agility**    | Fine‚Äëgrained audit trails ‚Üî friction for authors.                                   |
| **Lane purity vs synthesis** | Keep TA/VA/LA separable ‚Üî publish a unified justification surface.                  |
| **Notation independence**    | Semantics in prose/math ‚Üî teams want diagrams/tables (informative only).            |
| **Design vs run**            | Design‚Äëtime evidence assembly ‚Üî run‚Äëtime telemetry ingestion must not be conflated. |
| **Crossings and planes**     | Crossings must be explicit and penalised correctly ‚Üî authors want ‚Äújust reuse it‚Äù.  |

#### G.6:4.2 - EvidenceGraph (object; kit‚Äëowned surface)

**Definition (object).** An `EvidenceGraph` is a **typed DAG** whose nodes are resolvable to A.10 anchors/carriers and evidencing roles, and whose edges represent minimal, normative provenance relations suitable for audit and path citation.

* **Nodes.** Each node is an A.10‚Äëanchored evidence carrier or evidence role (e.g., a proof carrier, a measurement record carrier, a tool‚Äëqualification carrier). Nodes MUST remain grounded in A.10 anchors and MUST NOT introduce mereological structure (A.10 firewall).
  * **Node kinds (explicit; stable).** Nodes MUST have an explicit kind tag `nodeKind ‚àà {U.EvidenceRole, SymbolCarrier, TransformerRole, MethodDescription, Observation}` (as used in the existing Part‚ÄëG vocabulary), so downstream projections can remain notation‚Äëindependent and audit‚Äëcheckable.
  * **Extension pins.** Method‚Äëfamily‚Äëspecific pins (e.g., QD/OEE) MUST NOT be introduced as new ‚Äúcore node kinds‚Äù; they are carried as additional pins only when the relevant `GPatternExtension` is in use and are recorded on UTS PathCards / SCR projections as required by that extension.
* **Edges (minimal normative vocabulary).** The pattern admits a small set of provenance edges sufficient for audit:

  * `verifiedBy` (formal line),
  * `validatedBy` (empirical line),
  * `fromWorkSet` (run‚Äëtime trace provenance),
  * `happenedBefore` (temporal ordering),
  * `derivedFrom` (controlled derivation).
  * *(Informative only)* `usedCarrier`, `interpretedBy` MAY appear as authoring aids, but MUST NOT be relied on for conformance checks (their semantics remain non‚Äënormative in G.6).
    Additional narrative edges MAY exist as informative annotations but MUST NOT be relied on for conformance checks.
* **Lane tags.** Every binding on a path is lane‚Äëtyped with `assuranceUse ‚àà {TA, VA, LA}` (lane separation remains explicit through to SCR projections; no silent cross‚Äëlane averaging).
* **Externality (no self‚Äëevidence).** Any evidencing `TransformerRole` that would certify the evaluated holon MUST be modelled as external (or model a meta‚Äëholon explicitly); G.6 does not permit reflexive ‚Äúself‚Äëevidence‚Äù shortcuts.
* **Context and plane attachment.** Nodes and claims carry `BoundedContext` and `ReferencePlane`. Any movement across context/kind/plane/design‚Üîrun/edition boundaries is represented via explicit GateCrossing/CrossingBundle artefacts (with crossing pins routed per `G.Core`).

#### G.6:4.3 - PathId and PathSliceId (citable justification addresses)

**PathId (address for justifications).** A `PathId` is a stable identifier minted for a **claim‚Äëlocal, lane‚Äëtyped** path in an `EvidenceGraph` under a declared scope slice (including a time selector where applicable) and a declared `ReferencePlane`. A `PathId` is meant to be citable from downstream artefacts (LOG, UTS, parity, shipping) without duplicating evidence tables.

A `PathId` citation surface SHALL include, at minimum:

* the lane split (TA/VA/LA) for the path,
* the explicit crossing pins (when crossings are traversed),
* the freshness/time attachment status for empirical legs (when present), including any explicit `validUntil`/expiry marker when one is declared (or a decay/freshness policy pin that implies expiry),
* the pinned policy identifiers relevant to the path‚Äôs penalty/trust wiring (policy ids are cited; policies remain owned elsewhere),
* the effective crossing‚Äëtrust ‚Äúbottleneck‚Äù information when crossings exist (e.g., lowest `CL`/`CL^k`/`CL^plane` encountered on the cited slice),
* the effective `Œì‚Äëfold` in force for any published/relied‚Äëupon `R_eff` projection (default or explicit override), and (when overridden) the cited CAL `ProofLedger` ids that justify the override,
* the `EvidenceGraphId` and enough addressability to resolve the path to SCR/RSCR anchors.

**PathSliceId (time‚Äë & plane‚Äëlifted snapshot).** A `PathSliceId` denotes a **release‚Äëquality snapshot key** for a path under explicit time/plane binding (e.g., window policy + `ReferencePlane`) and is intended as the address used when refresh/RSCR wants *path‚Äëgranular* recomputation.

*The universal definition of ‚Äúwhat kinds of changes force refresh‚Äù is owned by `G.Core` (typed trigger kinds). G.6 only makes the slice addressable and pin‚Äëcomplete.*

When downstream methods require additional edition/policy pins for reproducibility (e.g., archive/illumination/QD surfaces), such pins are specified by the relevant `GPatternExtension` module(s) and are treated as *required pins when that extension is used*.

#### G.6:4.4 - Assurance and legality binding (delegation‚Äëfirst; no shadow specs)

G.6 does not redefine B.3 or legality rules; it binds evidence paths to existing owners:

* **Assurance skeleton.** Lane separation and the `F/G/R` skeleton are as per B.3. Any statement about penalty routing or default Œì‚Äëfold is delegated to `G.Core` and the default‚Äëownership index (do not restate).
* **CAL linkage.** When a path claims a proof obligation or an override (e.g., an explicit Œì‚Äëfold override), it MUST cite the relevant CAL `ProofLedger` / `EvidenceProfiles` artefacts (G.4) rather than inventing local semantics.
* **Legality binding.** If a path includes numeric comparisons/aggregations, the legality surface MUST be *cited* via `CG‚ÄëSpec` (G.0) rather than re‚Äëimplemented in G.6 prose.

#### G.6:4.5 - Conceptual interface (notation‚Äëindependent surface; informative shapes)

These are conceptual shapes, not tool APIs (E.5 discipline).

* `Explain(pathId | pathSliceId)` ‚Üí returns a citation‚Äëready explanation bundle: lane split, relevant pins (crossings/policies/editions), freshness binding, and links to contributing anchors (A.10) and any CAL evidence/profile refs.
* `PathsFor(claim, scopeSlice, referencePlane)` ‚Üí enumerates admissible paths, returning `PathId[]` with enough metadata to support selection/audit queries.
* `Snapshot(pathId | pathSliceId)` ‚Üí emits a release‚Äëgrade snapshot record (SCR/RSCR‚Äëgrade) whose keys are citable and whose pins are explicit.

### G.6:7.5 - Interfaces & Hooks (normative)

Each hook below defines: **Trigger ‚Üí Obligation ‚Üí Publishes/Consumes ‚Üí Invariants**.
Where universal invariants apply (crossings, penalties, trigger typing), this section *cites* `G.Core` rather than redefining semantics.

#### G.6:7.5.1 - H1 ‚Äî UTS Name Card for Evidence Artefacts

* **Trigger.** A new EvidenceGraph node is minted (an A.10‚Äëanchored evidence artefact or role).
* **Obligation.** Mint a UTS Name Card with twin labels (Tech/Plain), citing the home context anchor and any required edition pins.
* **Publishes/Consumes.** Publishes: UTS row. Consumes: A.10 anchor metadata.
* **Invariants.** UTS publication and any deprecation/aliasing follow `G.Core` routing to F.17 (UTS discipline).

#### G.6:7.5.2 - H2 ‚Äî UTS PathCard (PathId/PathSliceId)

* **Trigger.** A new `PathId` (or `PathSliceId`) is minted.
* **Obligation.** Publish a UTS PathCard with twin labels, listing the explicit pins required by ¬ß4.1 (context/plane/time binding, crossing pins if any). If an extension requires additional pins for reproducibility (e.g., `G.6:Ext.QD_OEE_TelemetryPins`), those pins MUST be present when the extension is in use.
* **Publishes/Consumes.** Publishes: UTS row(s). Consumes: EvidenceGraph path metadata + any extension‚Äërequired pins.
* **Invariants.** Crossing visibility and penalty routing are delegated to `G.Core` (`CC‚ÄëGCORE‚ÄëCROSS‚Äë1`, `CC‚ÄëGCORE‚ÄëPEN‚Äë1`).

#### G.6:7.5.3 - H3 ‚Äî RSCR Trigger on Evidence‚ÄëImpacting Edit (typed; alias‚Äëdockable)

* **Trigger.** Any edit in G.6 that can change a path‚Äôs audit‚Äërelevant surface (evidence structure, crossing pins, penalty policy pins, plane binding, freshness binding, edition/policy pins, or telemetry‚Äëbound fields).
* **Obligation.** Emit RSCR triggers **using canonical `RSCRTriggerKindId`** (from `G.Core`) and record affected scope (`PathId/PathSliceId`) plus payload pins required for downstream refresh. If a legacy `G.6:H3:*` label is recorded, it is recorded as an alias label and docked via `G.Core.TriggerAliasMap.G6`. ### G.6:8 - Common Anti-Patterns and How to Avoid Them

* **Narrative‚Äëonly provenance (‚Äúbecause story‚Äù).**
  **Avoid:** mint `PathId/PathSliceId` and require citation for any decision that claims evidence‚Äëbased justification (CC‚ÄëG6‚Äë9).
* **Implicit crossings (‚Äúsame thing, different context‚Äù).**
  **Avoid:** represent crossings only via explicit crossing artefacts/pins; treat edition/plane/context changes as explicit crossing‚Äërelevant edits and trigger RSCR (core‚Äëowned crossing discipline).
* **Smuggling legality rules into EvidenceGraph prose.**
  **Avoid:** cite/pin legality surfaces (`CG‚ÄëSpec` and CAL artefacts); do not introduce local ‚Äúmini‚ÄëCG‚Äù rules in G.6 (route via `CC‚ÄëGCORE‚ÄëCN‚ÄëCG‚Äë1`).
* **Unpinned editions/policies (‚Äúit‚Äôs obvious which version‚Äù).**
  **Avoid:** require explicit edition/policy pins on citable paths; treat changes as typed triggers.
* **Alias‚Äëonly RSCR causes (‚ÄúH3: something changed‚Äù).**
  **Avoid:** record canonical `RSCRTriggerKindId` as the cause; aliases are labels only and must dock via `G.Core.TriggerAliasMap.G6`.

### G.6:9 - Consequences

**Benefits.** Path‚Äëaddressable provenance; crossing/plane effects are auditable by pins rather than folklore; selectors and auditors share the same object; refresh becomes localized (path‚Äëscoped) rather than global ‚Äúrerun everything‚Äù.
**Trade‚Äëoffs.** Authors must declare (or pin) time/plane/scope and keep pins explicit; mitigated by reusing CAL EvidenceProfiles and by modularizing method‚Äëspecific telemetry as Extensions.

### G.6:10 - Rationale

G.6 concretizes the ‚Äúbecause‚Äëgraph‚Äù implicit in A.10 into a typed, lane‚Äëaware DAG with stable path addresses. It relies on canonical owners for semantics:

* A.10 for anchoring discipline and carrier reality,
* B.3 for the assurance skeleton,
* G.4 for proof/evidence profile semantics,
* `G.Core` for universal crossing, penalty, default ownership, and typed RSCR cause discipline.

This preserves conceptual modularity: G.6 standardizes *addressable provenance*, not a competing legality or selection mechanism.

### G.6:11 - SoTA‚ÄëEchoing

This pattern aligns with post‚Äë2015 best practice in reproducibility and evaluation governance by:

* treating **provenance and versioning/pinning** as first‚Äëclass audit surfaces (rather than informal ‚Äúmethods‚Äù prose),
* enabling **selective re‚Äëevaluation** (path‚Äëscoped refresh) rather than global reruns whenever one policy/edition changes,
* separating **design‚Äëtime specifications** from **run‚Äëtime traces/telemetry**, matching modern reproducibility and ‚Äúlineage‚Äù practice in complex ML/scientific pipelines,
* keeping **method‚Äëfamily specifics** (e.g., archive/illumination/QD pins or open‚Äëended telemetry pins) modular via extension wiring instead of embedding them into the universal provenance core.
