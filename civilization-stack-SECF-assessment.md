# CIVILIZATION STACK BOOTSTRAP: SECF CAPABILITY DEVELOPMENT PLAN

## PREFACE

This document applies the Systems Engineering Competency Framework (SECF v2) to the Civilization Stack bootstrap problem: how does a community starting from absolute ground zero develop all 40 SECF competency areas?

**SECF Proficiency Levels:** Awareness → Supervised Practitioner → Practitioner → Lead Practitioner → Expert

**Bootstrap Stages:** Stage 0 (Manual, Yrs 1-3) → Stage 1 (Simple Machines, Yrs 3-10) → Stage 2 (CNC, Yrs 10-20) → Stage 3 (Advanced, Yrs 20-50)

**The Core Paradox:** Developing engineering competency requires competent engineers; developing engineers requires training; developing training requires competent engineers. Resolution: (1) Written materials first — OAD stores procedures from prior Practitioners. (2) Iterative approximation — each stage enables better tools → better training → better practitioners. (3) Federated learning — communities specialize and federate. (4) Simulation before practice.

**Critical Risk:** Loss of OAD written materials → collapse to Awareness level across all 40 competencies simultaneously.

---

## SECTION A: INTEGRAL AS COMPETENCY DEVELOPMENT SYSTEM

- **CDS** — Deliberates on priorities, manages curriculum, authorizes remediation
- **OAD** — Stores all training materials, procedures, failure analyses, curriculum revisions
- **ITC** — Tracks teaching, learning, proficiency demonstration as contributions
- **COS** — Allocates time and resources to competency development
- **FRS** — Monitors competency gaps, triggers CDS remediation

## SECTION B: ALL 40 COMPETENCY AREAS

### THEME 1: SYSTEMS FUNDAMENTALS

**1. Systems Thinking (ST)**
S0: Map simplest systems (fire, bloomery, compost) — boundaries, inputs, outputs, emergent properties. S1: Water wheel as teaching artifact — mechanical + hydraulic + human components. Map full system, failure modes. S2: System dynamics simulations. Model bootstrap investments. S3: Full community simulation, all 5 INTEGRAL subsystems as feedback loops.
Target: Practitioner (S2), Lead Practitioner (S3)

**2. Life Cycles (LC)**
S0: Natural cycles (seed → plant → harvest → compost). Map crop life cycles, identify vulnerabilities. S1: Product life cycles for tools (knife: ore → forged → used → sharpened → recycled). S2: Life cycle cost assessment for major investments. S3: Full community life cycle management — competency succession planning.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**3. Capability Engineering (CE)**
S0: Define tool capabilities operationally ("knife cuts 2cm hardwood, 100 cuts"). Test and document. S1: Infrastructure capability definitions with operational envelopes (normal, stress, emergency). S2: Capability-based acquisition for complex systems. S3: Full capability matrix for all systems.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**4. General Engineering (GE)**
S0: Prioritize fundamentals for Stage 0 production — thermodynamics of combustion, chemistry of smelting, material properties. Empirical focus. S1: Load calculations, power calculations, heat transfer calculations. Templates in OAD. S2: Structural analysis, fluid dynamics, thermodynamics simulations. S3: Full engineering analysis for complex systems.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**5. Critical Thinking (CT)**
S0: Evidence-based decision-making in CDS. Every claim requires OAD records, observed data, or explicit reasoning. Assertion vs. evidence distinction. S1: Hypothesis testing for technical claims. S2: Evaluating complex designs, identifying hidden assumptions. S3: Meta-critical thinking — evaluating whether decision processes produce good outcomes.
Target: Practitioner (all stages)

### THEME 2: SE PROCESSES

**6. Systems Modeling (SM)**
S0: Paper-and-pencil system diagrams, equations, causal chains. Model verification. S1: Physical scale models (e.g., water wheel efficiency test). S2: Spreadsheet simulations. S3: Full model-based systems engineering in OAD.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**7. Communications (CM)**
S0: Written standards — OAD entries, CDS decisions, technical procedures. Feedback and objection norms. S1: Technical reports, peer review. S2: Formal processes — change control, design review. S3: Federated communication across communities and languages.
Target: Practitioner (all stages)

**8. Requirements Definition (RD)**
S0: Practice capability-based specifications. Test against spec. S1: Formal requirements for infrastructure. S2: Full requirements engineering — stakeholder needs to component requirements traceability. S3: Federated requirements management.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**9. System Architecting (SA)**
S0: Study pre-collapse examples in OAD. Read drawings and schematics. Reproduce and modify known designs. S1: Design with patterns — serial, parallel, feedback. Apply to community systems. S2: Modularity, hierarchy, layering. Design for incremental upgrade. S3: Systems of systems, federated architectures. INTEGRAL as meta-reference.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**10. Design For... (DF)**
S0: Design for simplicity — fewest parts meeting capability definition. Most producible with available tools. S1: Design for reliability — failure modes, redundancy. S2: Design for maintainability — threaded fasteners, modular assemblies, design for disassembly. S3: Design for sustainability — full life cycle assessment, local material renewability, end-of-life recycling.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**11. Integration (IN)**
S0: Integration checklist — does each component work independently? Interface specs defined? Incremental test — one component at a time. S1: Integration plan — sequence, test equipment, acceptance criteria. S2: Formal integration with change control. S3: Federated integration across communities. ICDs.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**12. Interfaces (IF)**
S0: Name every interface. Document: what connects to what, dimensions, materials. S1: ICDs — mechanical, electrical, informational. Version-controlled in OAD. S2: Interface verification testing. Maintain verification log. S3: Federated interface management. ICD negotiation.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**13. Verification (VF)**
S0: Verification by examination — does it meet spec? Destructive testing of samples for critical items. S1: Formal verification procedures — method, equipment, pass criteria. In OAD. S2: Verification planning before building. S3: Full verification program — matrix, traceability, formal records.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**14. Validation (VL)**
S0: Distinguish verification (built correctly?) from validation (right thing built?). Does the knife actually cut what it needs in actual use? S1: Structured validation plan — stakeholder needs, use context, success criteria. S2: Operational validation in realistic conditions. S3: Federated validation across communities.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**15. Transition (TR)**
S0: Before operational use — document: how to use, maintenance needs, failure signs, responsible party. S1: Formal transition plans for infrastructure — operator training, manuals, spare parts, maintenance schedule. S2: Complex system transition management. Phased with verification. S3: Federated transition — training transfer, localization, support setup.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**16. Utilization and Support (US)**
S0: Maintenance culture — clean tools, store properly, inspect before use. Maintenance log. S1: Scheduled maintenance — wear patterns, intervals, procedures in OAD. S2: Full support system — scheduling, inventory, training, records. S3: Predictive maintenance — FRS sensors predict needs before failure.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**17. Retirement (RT)**
S0: On retirement — document: replacement, component disposition, knowledge to preserve. S1: Formal retirement plans — criteria, process, disposition, knowledge preservation. S2: Full retirement documentation. Component reuse/recycling planned before build. S3: Federated retirement — knowledge transfer protocols.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

### THEME 3: MANAGEMENT

**18. Planning (PL)**
S0: Every significant task has a written plan — what, order, when, resources. Paper Gantt charts. S1: Formal project planning — WBS, duration estimates, dependencies, critical path. S2: Integrated master schedule across all projects. Resource leveling. S3: Federated planning across communities.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**19. Monitoring and Control (MC)**
S0: Weekly progress review — did we do what we planned? If not, why? Next week's plan? S1: Earned value tracking — milestones, assessment, variance. S2: Full project monitoring — schedule tracking, variance analysis, corrective action. S3: Federated monitoring with FRS real-time data.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**20. Decision Management (DM)**
S0: Every significant decision documented — what, why, who, objections. S1: Decision criteria before deciding. Record criteria and option evaluations. S2: Decision tracking — revisit and revise based on outcomes. Queryable OAD log. S3: Federated decision rights — local vs. cluster vs. regional decisions.
Target: Practitioner (all stages)

**21. Concurrent Engineering (CE)**
S0: Identify parallelizable activities — while forge heats, prepare ore. Apply to daily work. S1: Parallel project planning — identify critical path, schedule parallel tasks to finish before needed. S2: Full concurrent engineering — overlapped design, manufacturing, testing. S3: Federated concurrent engineering across communities.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**22. Business Enterprise Integration (BE)**
S0: Identify existing processes — procurement, production, distribution, value tracking. Document information flow. S1: Formal process descriptions — owner, inputs, outputs, interfaces. S2: Process integration through information systems. OAD as integration mechanism. S3: Federated business integration through shared OAD and ITC.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**23. Acquisition and Supply (AS)**
S0: Collection documentation — what, where, when, condition. S1: Formal procurement — internal allocation and inter-community trade. Document criteria and decisions. S2: Supply chain management — relationships, inventory, lead times. FRS alerts. S3: Federated acquisition through ITC. CDS authorization.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**24. Information Management (IM)**
S0: Information inventory — what we produce, who uses it, how stored. Eliminate redundancy. S1: Structured IM — naming, folder structure, version discipline, access control. S2: Full IM system — metadata standards, information flow diagrams, lifecycle management. S3: Federated IM — shared standards, local confidentiality respected.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**25. Configuration Management (CMgt)**
S0: Name things consistently. Version OAD entries. S1: Formal CM — CIs, baselines, change control. CDS approves significant changes. S2: Full CM — change request, impact analysis, configuration audit, traceability. S3: Federated CM — shared baselines, cross-community change triggers.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**26. Risk Management (RM)**
S0: After-action reviews — why did it fail? What else could have happened? OAD failure analysis. S1: Proactive risk identification — what could go wrong? Impact? Risk register. S2: Full risk management — probability/impact assessment, response planning (mitigate/transfer/accept/avoid), monitoring. S3: Federated risk management across communities.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**27. Project Management (PM)**
S0: Treat every significant undertaking as a project — scope, plan, track, adjust. S1: Formal PM — charter (CDS authorized), WBS, schedule, budget, risk register, status reporting. S2: Full PM — earned value, integrated change control, retrospective (lessons learned in OAD). S3: Federated PM for multi-community projects.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**28. Finance (Finance)**
S0: Resource tracking — what we have, allocated to what, available. Simple OAD ledger. S1: Resource allocation — prioritize competing demands. CDS deliberates major allocations. S2: Full financial management — budgets, variance, forecasting, investment analysis. S3: Federated financial management through ITC.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**29. Logistics (Logistics)**
S0: Basic logistics — where things stored, who retrieves, how transported. Document locations and methods. S1: Formal logistics — inventory management, reorder points, transportation planning, delivery scheduling. S2: Full logistics management — supply chain, distribution network, logistics information. S3: Federated logistics — shared transportation across communities.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**30. Quality (Quality)**
S0: Quality = meeting the specification. Verify built items meet specs. Fix or update spec. S1: Quality management — inspection, nonconformance reporting, corrective action, PDCA cycle. S2: Full QMS — quality planning, assurance, control, improvement. S3: Federated QMS — shared standards, cross-community quality learning.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

### THEME 4: PEOPLE DYNAMICS

**31. Ethics and Professionalism (Ethics)**
S0: Establish ethical norms through CDS deliberation. Document in OAD. Core norms: honesty, reliability, fairness, respect. S1: Apply ethics to technical decisions — safety, environment, people. Document reasoning. S2: Professional ethics — codes of conduct, ethical review for significant decisions. S3: Federated ethics — negotiate shared ethical standards across communities.
Target: Practitioner (all stages)

**32. Technical Leadership (TL)**
S0: Lead by example — best questions, clearest thinking, clearest OAD writing. S1: Identify technical direction, build consensus, mentor junior members. S2: Multi-project coordination. S3: Federated technical leadership across communities.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac-Expert (S3)

**33. Negotiation (Neg)**
S0: Basic negotiation practice in CDS — work through disagreements. Document process and outcome. S1: Formal negotiation — interest-based (separate interests from positions, generate options, use objective criteria). S2: Complex multi-party negotiations, inter-community negotiations. S3: Federated negotiation — agreements between communities, dispute resolution.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**34. Team Dynamics (TD)**
S0: Team norms — how we decide, how we handle conflict, how we give feedback. In OAD. S1: Role clarity — who responsible for what, expectations, proactive role conflict management. S2: Full team development — forming, storming, norming, performing. S3: Multi-team coordination across communities.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**35. Facilitation (Fac)**
S0: Identify natural facilitators. Give basic training. S1: Formal facilitation — all significant CDS deliberations. Neutral on content, manages process. S2: Complex facilitation — multi-day workshops, conflict resolution. S3: Federated facilitation — across communities, cultural differences.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**36. Emotional Intelligence (EI)**
S0: Emotional awareness — recognize and name emotions present in community. S1: Emotional regulation — strategies for managing emotions that interfere with judgment. S2: Empathy in technical contexts — understanding how decisions affect people's emotions. S3: EI at scale — emotional dynamics in federated diverse communities.
Target: Practitioner (all stages)

**37. Coaching and Mentoring (CM)**
S0: Apprenticeship is coaching — defined relationship, expectations, feedback. S1: Formal coaching skills — questioning techniques, feedback delivery (SBI), coaching conversations. S2: Full programs — structured mentoring, coaching plans, coaching circles. S3: Federated coaching — cross-community mentoring. Expert practitioners mentor across boundaries.
Target: SPrac (S1), LeadPrac (S2), Expert (S3)

### THEME 5: KNOWLEDGE MANAGEMENT

**38. Data (Data)**
S0: Data collection for critical decisions — record data, not just decision. S1: Systematic data collection — instrument key systems, maintain logs, define quality standards. S2: Data analysis — statistics, trends, visualization. Use data to inform decisions. S3: Federated data management — shared standards, data sharing with confidentiality protection.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**39. Knowledge Management (KM)**
S0: OAD is the KM system. Every significant piece of knowledge written in OAD. Knowledge only in someone's head is at risk. S1: Structured KM — categories, capture processes (after-action reviews, lessons learned), retrieval systems. S2: Full KMS — knowledge mapping (who knows what), gap analysis, sharing incentives (ITC). S3: Federated KM — contributions to and drawing from federated knowledge network.
Target: SPrac (S1), Practitioner (S2), LeadPrac (S3)

**40. Specialty Engineering (Domain)**
Domain-specific expertise (metallurgy, agriculture, medicine, manufacturing, energy, computation). Developed in parallel with all other competencies through the civilization-stack-ground-zero.md bootstrap pathways. Target varies by domain — see civilization-stack-ground-zero.md for domain-specific detail.

## SECTION D: CONCLUSION

The bootstrap paradox is real but not insurmountable. Start with written materials (OAD), practice with supervision (SPrac), build through repetition (Practitioner), contribute back (Lead Practitioner/Expert).

The SECF framework provides the map. civilization-stack-ground-zero.md provides the domain knowledge. INTEGRAL provides the coordination infrastructure. The bootstrap sequence provides the timeline.

**Build Critical Thinking first. Everything else follows.**
