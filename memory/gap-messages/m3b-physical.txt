<b>Gap 14: Microgrid islanding community energy</b>

<b>Core insight:</b> Islanding requires: generation, storage, smart load shedding, synchronization control.

<b>Architecture:</b>
• <b>Generation:</b> Solar + wind + microhydro (if water available)
• <b>Storage:</b> LiFePO4 batteries + flywheel for short-term
• <b>Inverter:</b> Grid-forming mode (not grid-following)
• <b>Control:</b> Open source microgrid controller (Naval Postgraduate School)
• <b>Load priority:</b> Critical (refrigeration, medical, comms) → Deferrable (heating, hot water) last

<b>Islanding Procedure:</b>
1. Detect grid failure (voltage/frequency anomaly)
2. Open main disconnect
3. Start grid-forming inverters (sync to 60Hz)
4. Connect critical loads first
5. Gradually add deferrable loads
6. Reconnect only when stable

<b>Products:</b> Schneider EcoStruxure, SMA Sunny Island (grid-forming capable)

<b>Gap 15: Aquaponics community food system design</b>

<b>Core insight:</b> Fish ammonia → bacteria convert to nitrate (plant fertilizer) → plants consume → clean water returns to fish.

<b>System Sizing (50 people):</b>
• 1lb fish per 10 gallons water
• 1lb fish needs ~1 sq ft growing area (NFT channels)
• 50g protein/person/day → 5lb/day production → 50lb stock → 500 gallons → 50 sq ft

<b>Components:</b>
• Fish tank: 500gal HDPE barrel, 75–85°F (tilapia)
• Biofilter: Moving bed or flood-and-drain
• Plant beds: DWC rafts or NFT channels
• Solids removal: Settling tank
• Water testing: pH, ammonia, nitrite, nitrate (daily)

<b>Economics:</b> ~$5K–10K | ~5lb fish/week + constant vegetables | 2–3yr payback
