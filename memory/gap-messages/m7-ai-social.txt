Sacred Clown = truth-teller who can say what others cannot. Referenced 132 times but not formalized.

<b>Formalization:</b>
• <b>Term:</b> Time-limited (1 year, renewable once)
• <b>Selection:</b> Self-nomination + CDS consent vote
• <b>Training:</b> Shadow existing Sacred Clown for 3 months
• <b>Authority:</b> Can call "emperor has no clothes" moment — pauses deliberation, forces re-examination
• <b>Accountability:</b> After using authority → write reflection on what happened and why

<b>Theatrical/Ritual Elements:</b>
• Initiation ceremony (sacred items, story)
• Costume (specific to role)
• Signature phrase or gesture

<b>Gap 35: De-ritualized healing community protocol</b>

<b>Core insight:</b> Trauma is stored in body and relationships. Healing happens through: witnessing, naming, shared story, new experience.

<b>Protocol (8 steps):</b>
1. <b>Initiate:</b> Person asks for circle (via Sacred Clown, coordinator, or friend)
2. <b>Gather:</b> 4–8 people, circle formation, no eye contact required
3. <b>Opening:</b> Simple intention statement — "We gather to support [name]"
4. <b>Story:</b> Person speaks their experience (no interruption, no advice)
5. <b>Witness:</b> Others say what they heard — "I heard you say..."
6. <b>Integration:</b> Others share similar experiences (resonance, not advice)
7. <b>Closing:</b> Simple statement — "You are not alone"
8. <b>Follow-up:</b> Check-in at 1 week, 1 month

<b>Facilitator Training:</b> Mental health peer support (80 hours)
