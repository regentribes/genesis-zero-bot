# Venture Family: A Better Way to Build Companies

**Author:** Victor Vorski (shared via LinkedIn, April 2026)

## Core Concept
A Venture Family is a group of regenerative organisations with shared purpose, culture, and long-term vision that join to form:
- A community of care
- A community of learning & development
- A community of action

Each venture retains its own company and legal structure. The family shares culture, purpose, fractional staff, support teams, customers, projects, and technologies where it makes sense. They execute collective fundraising.

**Key inversion from venture studio:** A venture studio brings benefits of shared intelligence/functions/services but in a *top-down* structure. A Venture Family takes the same benefits but inverts into a *bottom-up circle* — a self-organised cooperative of companies. Unlike a studio or incubator, membership is "for life" of the organisations, enabling long-term relationships of mentorship, eldership, and support.

## Why It Matters: Problems with Current Models
- Traditional startup models rely on "super-hero founders" and toxic culture of sacrifice/burnout
- For-purpose startups make this *worse* — purpose makes it easier to sacrifice healthy working culture
- Business models outside mainstream extractive narratives get harder access to funding/resources
- Founder burnout, founder conflict, poor mental health = major causes of startup failure
- **Core claim:** It is simply impossible to create a regenerative organisation stand-alone

## The 6 Levels of a Regenerative Organisation
(A regenerative org must thrive at all 6 levels; doing this in one org is impossible)

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 6. Inter-ecosystem | Global — economy, biosphere, etc. |
| 5. Inner-ecosystem | Network of connected organisations (your ecosystem) |
| 4. Outer-organisational | Stakeholders, capitals, incorporation (non-human person) |
| 3. Inner-organisational | Roles, work processes, organisation design |
| 2. Inter-personal | Relationships, culture, interactions between people |
| 1. Inner-personal | Identity, inner voices, meaning-making, thoughts/actions |

*Framework from Graham Boyd*

## Building Blocks of a Venture Family

### Shared Purpose / North Star
Uniting the Family — shared vision of the future being created

### Shared Culture
Portfolio Fundraise — shared fundraising through portfolio approach, with a whole-family holding structure that has 100% of direct financial investment equity

### Community of Care
System of mutual support for all leaders and team members. Shared "HR" — building shared culture and human systems.

### Community of Development & Learning
Venture Family helps each other grow, learn together, build collective wisdom. Share resources to tap opportunities and protect against unknowable losses.

### Build In Public
Natural communication flow for outer-organisational, inner-ecosystem, and inter-ecosystem connections.

### Community of Action
Shared Services (communications, facilitation), Shared Technologies, Shared Communications (re-sharing, cross-promotion)

## Family Life Spheres
A Venture Family integrates across all spheres — some families richer in certain areas, but some degree of sharing needed in each:
- **Purpose** — vision of the world being created
- **People** — culture, values, spirituality, personal/professional development, fractional staff, flexible resource sharing
- **Business** — communications, market research, sales, customers, partners, customer support
- **Product** — interoperability, shared standards
- **Technology** — shared tech stacks

## Venture Family vs. Other Models

| Feature | Venture Family | Venture Studio | Incubator | Accelerator |
|---------|---------------|----------------|-----------|------------|
| Definition | Cooperative of companies sharing care, learning, action | Builds startups in-house from scratch | Nurtures early-stage with resources/mentorship | Intensive program to grow fast |
| Funding | Portfolio fundraise; portion to core for shared services | Fully funds ($500k-$5M+), takes majority equity | Minimal or none | $50k-$500k for 5-10% equity |
| Equity | Depends on fundraise; ecosystem holding co. has 100% investment equity | 30-80% (majority) | Under 5% | 5-10% |
| Duration | Life of venture | Years per startup | Open-ended, 1-3 years | 3-6 months |
| Stage | Whole life-cycle | No startup yet — built from scratch | Idea/early stage | MVP + looking to scale |
| Membership | For life | N/A (studio owns companies) | Time-bound program | Time-bound program |

**Key distinction:** Venture Family members join for life; the goal is long-term stable relationship extending to any offspring ventures the venture has.
