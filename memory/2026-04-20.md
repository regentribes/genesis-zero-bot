### Ben Goertzel on Learning
Three kinds of learning current ML doesn't do:
1. Continual learning — humans learn without catastrophic forgetting; current neural nets forget everything when they learn new
2. Transfer learning — humans leap out-of-distribution; LLMs appear to transfer but actually just saw both domains in training
3. Developmental/lifeless learning — humans restructure how they represent knowledge over lifetime; current nets don't do this at all

Core: modular + abstracted knowledge representation enables all three. Logic systems support this naturally but don't scale to perceptual data. Hybrid: neural for perception, symbolic for reasoning, unified in knowledge graph. Hyperon/Atomspace. Predictive coding and causal coding as alternatives to backpropagation.
