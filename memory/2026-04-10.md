# 2026-04-10 — Airic Easm conversation

## Thread: Political inflation, grey eminences, and belonging

Key frames developed with Airic:

### Political Inflation
- Proliferation of political actors deflates the real value of political authority
- Sovereign citizen movement as "currency collapse" — search for something "hard" when fiat loses trust
- Natural law, state nationals, physical land = the "gold standard" people seek in a debased system

### Grey Eminences Working in Deflation
- People who operate in the space where political currency has collapsed
- Building actual infrastructure (cooperative governance, land trusts, mutual aid, food systems) vs. political theater
- The real question: descriptive (who holds power) or prescriptive (where should meaningful workers locate themselves)

### Opening Access to Deflation Culture
- Apprenticeship over membership — entry through contribution, not credential
- Reputation portability — vouching systems, demonstrated work, not certificates
- Distributed networks that recognize each other's vouching rather than one centralized open structure

### The Storytelling Problem
- Sovereign citizens tell compelling escape/return narratives
- Regen communities have the mechanisms but tell boring/preachy stories
- Show texture: negotiation, failures, boring coordination work
- Refuse the savior frame — people doing things together

### The Belonging Question (captured as primary design problem)
- "How do you make people feel like they can enter mid-stream and still belong?"
- Regen communities: **high floor, unclear ceiling** (too much prerequisites, never clear when you're "in")
- Sovereign citizens: **low floor, infinite ceiling** (vocabulary IS the entry point, always more rabbit holes)
- Design solution:
  - Low floor: show up, do work, no belief required
  - Clear next action: always something concrete now
  - Hospitality as structure: burden on community to make space
  - Ongoing story: we are *building this*, here's where we are, where do you fit?
