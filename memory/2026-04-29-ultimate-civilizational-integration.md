## II. The Complete Context Map

Across all threads, the following frameworks and contexts exist simultaneously:

### A. The Integral Cooperative Economy

```
CDS → OAD → COS → ITC → FRS → CDS
```

**CDS (Collaborative Decision System):** How the community decides what to do. Not a vote. Not a hierarchy. Structured deliberation that surfaces objections, finds common ground, makes decisions everyone can live with.

**OAD (Open Access Design):** How the community allocates resources toward outcomes. Global repository of designs adapted to local context. Ecological footprint calculation.

**COS (Coordination and Operations System):** How the community decomposes work, matches skills to tasks, schedules workflow.

**ITC (Input-Output Tracking):** How the community tracks contributions (time, skills, materials, trust) and outcomes (value created).

**FRS (Feedback and Response System):** How the community monitors whether what it builds is actually working, detects anomalies, and routes signals back to CDS for deliberation.

## VI. The Federation Multiplier (Why It Matters for Civilizational Rebuild)

### The Core Insight

A single community cannot rebuild civilization alone. The technology trees show this clearly:

- Some ore deposits exist in some regions but not others
- Some climates support certain crops but not others
- Some communities have smithing traditions, others have ceramics traditions
- Some have nearby sources of copper, tin, lithium, or rare earths

**The federation multiplies capability through trade and knowledge sharing.**

### What Federation Enables

**OAD design sharing:** Community A's water catchment designs benefit Community B. Community B's bloomery designs benefit Community A. The OAD repository becomes shared infrastructure.

**Specialization:** Each community focuses on what it can produce most efficiently given its local resources, then trades for what it cannot produce.

**Risk distribution:** If one community's crops fail, trade with other communities provides resilience. If one community's ore body is depleted, trade with other communities continues.

**Knowledge acceleration:** Learning from other communities' failures and successes accelerates the technology tree. No community has to repeat every mistake.

### The Federation Stack

| Federation Layer | What It Enables |
|---|---|
| OAD Design Sharing | Designs flow between communities, adapted to local context |
| COS Cross-Training | Skills transfer: one community teaches another |
| ITC Trade Protocols | Surplus goods and materials exchange between communities |
| FRS Threat Sharing | Threat intelligence shared: SCOPs, environmental hazards, resource depletion |
| CDS Deliberation | Inter-community governance decisions: shared infrastructure, resource allocation |
