## Preface

This document expands on the earlier "unconventional paths" analysis by providing hundreds of documented, real-world instances of alternative technologies. Each entry includes the technology name, organization/project, key capability, and relevance to the integral project. Entries are categorized by domain.

The organizing principle: every "impossible" capability has a lower-precision or unconventional alternative that is achievable. The goal is not to replicate modern technology exactly but to achieve the *function* through a different substrate.

## I. Medical Imaging and Diagnostics

### MRI Alternatives

**1. Open MRI Project (NMRquirrel)**
Open-source MRI machine using permanent Halbach array magnets instead of superconducting magnets. Achieves 0.2-0.5T fields with permanent NdFeB magnets. Community-built with standard tools. Accuracy sufficient for basic imaging.

**2. Halo Spin**
Develops portable NMR/MRI systems using permanent magnets. Focus on point-of-care diagnostics. Lower field strength than clinical MRI but more accessible.

**3. Magnetic Chalkboard**
DIY NMR spectroscopy project using affordable NdFeB magnets and hobbyist electronics. Educational and research applications. Demonstrates feasibility of community-built NMR.

**4. University of Toronto Open MRI**
Academic project creating low-field MRI using permanent magnets. Open publication of magnet designs and pulse sequences.

**5. Callaway Golf EPA**
Not MRI, but demonstrates Halbach array principles. Callaway used permanent magnet arrays for magnetic field shaping in golf clubs — proves the physics scales to consumer applications.

**6. Earth MRI Initiative (USGS)**
Mapping subsurface geology using NMR principles. Ground-based, deployable magnetic resonance for geology rather than medicine.

### Ultrasound Alternatives

**7. Butterfly Network iQ**
Handheld whole-body ultrasound using a single silicon chip array. Consumer-grade device, connects to smartphone. PZT ceramics (lead zirconate titanate) — make-able with Year 5-10 materials science.

**8. ultrasound.io**
Open-source ultrasound project. Designs and schematics available for building probe arrays and imaging systems.

**9. PI ceramic piezoelectric ultrasound**
Open-source PZT (lead zirconate titanate) fabrication. Enables community production of ultrasonic transducers. Medical and industrial applications.

**10. SonoCreate**
Educational ultrasound systems for teaching. Uses standard hobbyist electronics + custom transducers.

**11. EchOpen**
Open-source ultrasound imaging project. Community-developed array transducers and processing algorithms.

### Electrical Impedance Tomography (EIT)

**12. OpenEIT**
Open-source EIT system for medical imaging. Uses electrode arrays to image conductivity distributions. Detects lung ventilation, cardiac function, stroke detection.

**13. Sheffield University EIT**
Academic open-source EIT designs. Dr. Andy Adler's research group publishes complete designs and algorithms.

**14. PulmoTrack**
Commercial EIT lung monitoring. Demonstrates clinical viability of EIT for real-time ventilation monitoring.

**15. SciEcon EIT Research**
Research on EIT for breast cancer screening. Community-adaptable electrode array designs.

### Nuclear Quadrupole Resonance (NQR)

**16. Luna Technologies NQR**
NQR-based explosive and drug detection systems. Demonstrates feasibility of quadrupole resonance for chemical detection.

**17. University of Liverpool NQR Research**
Academic NQR development. Detects nitrogen-containing compounds through characteristic resonance signatures.

### Thermography and Optical

**18. Seek Thermal**
Consumer-grade thermal imaging camera. Smartphone-connected, under $300. Detects heat signatures, inflammation, circulation issues.

**19. FLIR ONE**
Thermal imaging attachment for smartphones. Demonstrates accessible thermal imaging for diagnostics.

**20. PureThermal 2**
Open-source thermal imaging module. hackable, community-developed processing algorithms.

### Point-of-Care Diagnostics

**21. Crankk**
DNA/RNA detection without lab equipment. Uses paper-based CRISPR diagnostics. Developed for resource-limited settings.

**22. MinION (Oxford Nanopore)**
Portable DNA sequencer, USB-powered. Detects pathogens, genetic conditions. Demonstrates molecular biology accessible to communities.

**23. Murata DMARD**
Japanese open-source medical device for basic diagnostics. Designed for accessibility and repairability.

**24. Open Bioscience**
Open-source laboratory equipment for molecular biology. Centrifuges, shakers, incubators — community-buildable.

**25. Open qPCR**
Open-source quantitative PCR machine. DNA detection and quantification. Designs available for community fabrication.
