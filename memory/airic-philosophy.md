To become something is to stop being everything.
Once form appears:
It can decay
It can fail
It can be lost
Identity creates fragility.
This is why many philosophical systems treat attachment to identity as the root of suffering.

The state you’re pointing to is subtle.
Not:
pure openness (nothing manifests)
Not:
rigid identity (complete collapse)

Form that remembers the field.
Examples in nature again:
Neural plasticity: a brain with stable patterns but constant rewiring
Mycelial networks: organized yet constantly adapting
Immune memory: remembers threats but can generate new receptors
The system collapses repeatedly without freezing.

Release the identity of the collapse
Return to the field without fragmentation
In other words:
Fluid identity instead of fixed identity.
