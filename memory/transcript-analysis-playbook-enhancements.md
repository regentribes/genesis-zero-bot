## 1. Intelligence Definition
**Argument:** Intelligence = solving non-standard problems with non-standard methods using experience. Not just pattern-matching or processing.

**Playbook Enhancement → Areas 1 & 3:**
Add "Intelligence Profile" subsection — does the community value adaptive problem-solving, specialized expertise, creative reasoning, or pattern recognition?

---

## 2. Six Levels of Intelligence
1. Reflex (2-neuron instant response)
2. Conditional reflex (learned associations)
3. Instinct (pattern recognition, longer sequences)
4. "Super-instinct" (abstracted motivation)
5. Intelligence proper (novel solutions to novel problems)
6. "Super-intelligence" (predicting futures, multi-step planning)

**Playbook Enhancement → Areas 2 & 3:**
Add "Cognitive Tier Mapping" — map founding group across 6 levels. Identify gaps. Who handles reactive vs. adaptive vs. strategic tasks?

---

## 3. Human vs. Machine Intelligence
**Argument:** Human intelligence inseparable from limbic system (wants, fears, goals). Machine "intelligence" = crystallized neocortex without motivation. Speaker calls current AI "super-pigeons" — many specialized programs combined, but no goals.

**Playbook Enhancement → Areas 3 & 8:**
Add "Goal Architecture" section:
- Community's fundamental wants (survival, belonging, meaning, contribution)
- Individual vs. collective goals
- Stance on AI tools lacking goal architecture

---

## 4. Working Memory as Constraint
**Argument:** Working memory capacity (~4-7 chunks) is true limit on cognitive complexity. Even smart people can't hold 50 variables — must abstract, chunk, or use external tools.

**Playbook Enhancement → Areas 3 & 11:**
Add "Cognitive Load Design":
- Structure meetings within working memory limits
- External tools to augment collective cognition
- Prevent analysis paralysis from too much data

---

## 5. Intelligence as Information Reduction
**Argument:** Intelligence isn't gathering MORE information — it's FILTERING noise. Brain discards 99% of sensory input. True intelligence = knowing what to ignore.

**Playbook Enhancement → Areas 7 & 11:**
Add "Signal-to-Noise Design":
- How community filters information overload
- Decision frameworks that reduce noise
- Explicit "ignore list" — what signals are filtered

---

## 6. Collective Intelligence
**Argument:** Individual intelligence is limited — even smartest person can't run a village. Collective intelligence requires distributing cognition across roles, institutions, time.

**Playbook Enhancement → Areas 2 & 11:**
Add "Distributed Cognition Map":
- How is intelligence distributed across founding group?
- What roles handle what cognitive tasks?
- How is institutional memory preserved?

---

## 7. Social Archetypes
**Argument:** Different relationships to knowledge/power:
- Truth-seekers (academics)
- Reputation-seekers (social signalers)
- Power-seekers (controllers)
- Resource-seekers (economic actors)
- Force-seekers (coercion-based)

These groups often think others are "idiots."

**Playbook Enhancement → Areas 2 & 3:**
Add "Cognitive Archetype Assessment":
- What mix in founding group?
- Prevent single-archetype dominance
- Governance structures for different styles

---

## 8. Danger: Building AI Without Understanding Intelligence
**Argument:** Companies building "intelligent" systems without understanding intelligence itself. May create capability without comprehension — unintended consequences.

**Playbook Enhancement → Areas 1 & 3:**
Add "Technology Stance":
- Community's position on AI/automation
- AI for decision support?
- Maintain human agency as AI grows

---

## 9. Knowledge Transmission Bottleneck
**Argument:** Core problem is transmission — can't efficiently transfer knowledge mind-to-mind. Takes 20 years to train expert. We lack "CTRL+F5" for human knowledge.

**Playbook Enhancement → Areas 10 & 11:**
Add "Knowledge Transfer Systems":
- Document and transfer expertise
- "Before I die" list — critical knowledge held by few
- Create redundancy in critical areas
- Role of AI in knowledge management

---

## 10. Specialization vs. Generalization
**Argument:** Expertise in one domain doesn't transfer — brilliant physicist may be lost socially. Working memory limits apply within domains too.

**Playbook Enhancement → Areas 2 & 3:**
Add "T-Shaped Skills Model":
- Deep expertise per role
- Generalist overlap for coordination
- Prevent "domain silos"

---

## 11. Evolution of Intelligence
**Argument:** Intelligence evolution: crystals → chemistry → biology → nervous systems → animals → humans → institutions → AI. Each transition = new abstraction levels and goal-structure.

**Playbook Enhancement → Areas 1 & 3:**
Add "Intelligence Evolution Perspective":
- Where does community sit on this continuum?
- What intelligence layers does community embody?
- How relate to intelligence in non-human systems?
