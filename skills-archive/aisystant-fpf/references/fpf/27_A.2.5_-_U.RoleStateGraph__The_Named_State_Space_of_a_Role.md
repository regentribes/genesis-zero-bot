## A.2.5 - U.RoleStateGraph: The Named State Space of a Role

### A.2.5:1 - Purpose & scope (why this exists)

A **role** is not only a name; it is a *trajectory of admissible states* that governs when, and under which conditions, a holder of that role may **enact** steps of a `U.MethodDescription`. FPF therefore introduces a first‚Äëclass intensional object:

> **`U.RoleStateGraph` (RSG)** ‚Äî the **finite, named state space** of a **`U.Role` in a given `U.BoundedContext`**, with transitions guarded by conditions over the **Role Characterisation Space (RCS)** and contextual events.

The RSG is the **gate** between *assignment* (`U.RoleAssignment`) and *action* (`U.Work`). A step may be performed **only** when the performer‚Äôs assignment is **in an enactable RSG state** at the relevant **Window** (time slice) and this is **proven** by a contemporaneous **StateAssertion** (verdict of `U.Evaluation` against the state‚Äôs **Checklist**).


### A.2.5:2 - Problem frame (what goes wrong without an RSG)

1. **Readiness blur.** Teams conflate ‚Äúhas the badge‚Äù with ‚Äúis fit to act now‚Äù. Without explicit states (*Ready*, *Calibrated*, *Authorized*, *Suspended*‚Ä¶), enactment checks dissolve into ad‚Äëhoc judgement.
2. **Checklist drift.** Criteria for ‚Äúready/approved‚Äù live in scattered documents; there is no single conceptual anchor tying them to the role.
3. **Workflow/role confusion.** ‚ÄúState‚Äù of a *workflow* (according to workplan) is mistaken for the *state of a role* (eligibility to enact).
4. **Status ‚âÝ enactment.** Epistemic/Normative roles (e.g., *NormativeStandard*, *ApprovedSpecification*) need *statuses* that are **not enactable**, yet are used to gate decisions.
5. **Cross‚Äëcontext substitution by name.** Labels like *Approved* or *Ready* silently cross contexts with different criteria; the loss is hidden and unaudited.

**Consequences.** Violations of **Strict Distinction (A.7)** and **Didactic Primacy (E.12)**: ambiguous authority to act, unsafe SoD, and non‚Äëreproducible evaluations.


### A.2.5:3 - Core idea (didactic)

Think of a **Role** as a **mask**, and the **RSG** as the **traffic lights for that mask** inside one context of meaning.

* The **nodes** are **named states** (*Ready*, *Degraded*, *Suspended*, *Approved*, *Obsolete*‚Ä¶).
* The **edges** are **transitions** with **guards** (checkable conditions over RCS characteristics and contextual events, e.g., *CalibrationAge ‚â§ 30d*; *AuthorizationSpeechAct recorded*).
* Each **state** is paired with a **Checklist** (criteria you test to issue a **StateAssertion** for a given **Window**).
* Some states are **enactable = true** (green lights); others are **not enactable** (status lights) and therefore can **gate decisions** but **cannot** directly authorize `U.Work`.

> **One sentence.** **RSG says *when a badge is green*.** The Checklist proves it, the **StateAssertion** records it, and the Method step may proceed.


### A.2.5:4 - Minimal vocabulary (this pattern only)

* **`U.RoleStateGraph` (RSG).** Intensional object *owned by* `(Role, Context)`. Finite set of named **States** and typed **Transitions** with guards.
* **RSG.State.** Intensional **named place**. Properties:

  * `enactable ‚àà {true,false}` ‚Äî whether being in this state authorizes enactment of steps that require this role.
  * `initial?`, `terminal?` ‚Äî optional markers for lifecycle reasoning.
* **RSG.Transition.** Edge `state_i ‚Üí state_j` with **Guard** (predicate over RCS characteristics and/or contextual events such as `U.SpeechAct`, `U.Observation`, `U.Evaluation` results).
* **RCS (Role Characterisation Space).** The **characteristic bundle** that characterises this role in this Context (e.g., *CalibrationAge*, *AuthorizationScope*, *FatigueIndex*, *IndependenceFlag*, *EvidenceFreshness*). *(Defined in A.2 Role Taxonomy / RoleDescription.)*
* **State Checklist (description).** A **RoleDescription** component that enumerates **criteria** to test whether a holder can legitimately be treated as **in** a given state for a **Window**. *(Description, not the state itself.)*
* **`U.Evaluation` ‚Üí StateAssertion (verdict).** The **result** of applying the state‚Äôs Checklist to a **concrete holder** at a **time window**, yielding a **verdict** ‚ÄúIN‚ÄëSTATE(S) @Window‚Äù with provenance to observations/evidence.
* **Window.** Temporal interval to which the StateAssertion applies (e.g., `[2025‚Äë05‚Äë01, 2025‚Äë06‚Äë01]`).

> **Strict distinction note.**
>
> * **RSG** and its **States** are **intensionals** (what the role *is allowed to be*).
> * **Checklists** and **StateAssertions** are **descriptions/evaluations** (how we *know* a specific holder *is* in that state now).


### A.2.5:5 - What an RSG is **not** (guardrails)

* **Not a workflow.** RSG transitions do **not** encode task order; they encode **eligibility changes** of the *role*.
* **Not a capability list.** RSG is **authorization/readiness over time**, distinct from `U.Capability` (ability).
* **Not a global status set.** RSG lives **inside one Context**; the label *Ready* in another Context is **a different state** unless bridged (F.9).
* **Not a log.** RSG is not a history. Histories are **StateAssertions** over Windows; **`U.Work`** is the record of enactments.
* **Not a document lifecycle.** Epistemic role RSGs can *look like* document lifecycles, but they remain **role‚Äëstatus graphs**; the **carrier** lifecycle stays separate (A.7, `U.Carrier`).


### A.2.5:6 - Invariants (preview)

1. **Locality.** `RSG(Role, Context)` is defined **only** within that `U.BoundedContext`.
2. **Finiteness.** The **State** set is finite and named.
3. **Checklist pairing.** Every State has a **Checklist** in the Role‚Äôs **RoleDescription**; every enactable State has **at least one** observable criterion.
4. **Green‚Äëgate discipline.** A Method step requiring `Role` may proceed **only** if a contemporaneous **StateAssertion** exists for an **enactable** State.
5. **No silent Cross‚Äëcontext reuse.** Cross‚ÄëContext reuse requires a **Bridge** with CL and loss notes; local `‚ä•/‚â§/‚äó` always prevail.

### A.2.5:7 - Formal structure of an RSG (intensional, context‚Äëlocal)

> **Definition.** For a given **`U.Role`** in a given **`U.BoundedContext`**, its **`U.RoleStateGraph`** is the tuple
> `RSG(Role, Context) = ‚ü®S, S_en, T, Guard, init?‚ü©`, where:

* **`S`** ‚Äî a **finite set of named States** (`StateName ‚àà Tech register`, with a Plain label). Names are **local to `(Role, Context)`**.
* **`S_en ‚äÜ S`** ‚Äî the subset of **enactable** states (‚Äúgreen lights‚Äù). States in `S \ S_en` are **status‚Äëonly** (not enactable).
* **`T ‚äÜ S √ó S`** ‚Äî a set of **typed transitions** `s·µ¢ ‚Üí s‚±º`. Transitions are optional; the RSG may be acyclic or cyclic.
* **`Guard`** ‚Äî for each transition (and optionally for state maintenance), a **predicate over**:

  * the role‚Äôs **RCS snapshot** at a **Window** (values on named characteristics; see A.2.3), and
  * **Context events** (e.g., presence of a `U.SpeechAct`, freshness of `U.Observation`, validity of a prior `U.Evaluation`).
* **`init? : S ‚Üí {true,false}`** ‚Äî optionally marks **initial** state(s). (Useful for lifecycles; not required for gating.)

**Naming discipline (RSG‚ÄëN1‚Ä¶N3).**

1. **RSG‚ÄëN1 (Minimal set).** `|S| ‚â• 1`. At least **one** state must exist; if **no** state is enactable, the role is **status‚Äëonly** in this Context.
2. **RSG‚ÄëN2 (Disjoint labels).** State names are **unique** within `(Role, Context)`; reusing global labels (e.g., ‚ÄúReady‚Äù) across contexts is allowed **only** via Bridges (F.9).
3. **RSG‚ÄëN3 (Human scale).** For didactics, **‚â§ 7 states** is the default target; exceeding it requires a one‚Äësentence rationale (‚Äúdistinct gate we will actually use‚Äù).


### A.2.5:8 - Enactability & Checklist semantics (how a state is *known*, now)

An RSG **does not** determine history; it determines **what counts as being in a state**, and which states **authorize enactment**.

#### A.2.5:8.1 - State Checklists (description, not the state)

For each `s ‚àà S`, the **RoleDescription** (A.2.3) includes a **State Checklist** `Checklist(s)` ‚Äî a **named set of criteria** that can be evaluated at a **Window** to test ‚Äú**holder is in state `s`**‚Äù.

* **Criterion kinds (illustrative):**

  * **Threshold over RCS characteristic:** `CalibrationAge ‚â§ 30 days`.
  * **Presence of act:** `AuthorizationSpeechAct exists within 90 days`.
  * **Evidence freshness:** `Evidence(type=SafetyTest).age ‚â§ 12 months`.
  * **SoD flag:** `IndependenceFlag = true`.
  * **External status:** `StandardStatus = Approved`.

> **Strict distinction.** `Checklist(s)` is a **description**; the **state** `s` is an **intensional place** in the role‚Äôs RSG.

#### A.2.5:8.2 - From Checklist to **StateAssertion** (verdict of `U.Evaluation`)

Evaluating `Checklist(s)` at a **Window** produces an **`U.Evaluation` verdict**:

> **`StateAssertion(holder, Role, Context, s, Window)`** ‚Äî ‚Äú*For this Window, this holder **is** in state `s`*‚Äù, with provenance to the actual observations/evidence.

**Rules (RSG‚ÄëC1‚Ä¶C5).**

* **RSG‚ÄëC1 (All‚Äëmust‚Äëhold).** A `StateAssertion` **MUST** justify that **all required criteria** in `Checklist(s)` hold at the Window.
* **RSG‚ÄëC2 (Window freshness).** Each criterion **MUST** define its freshness window; if omitted, default is **instantaneous** at the Window‚Äôs end time.
* **RSG‚ÄëC3 (No guess).** Pure opinion is disallowed; every criterion is grounded in **observable facts** (`U.Observation`, `U.Work` record, `U.SpeechAct`, or a derived `U.Evaluation`).
* **RSG‚ÄëC4 (Non‚Äëmonotonic over time).** A `StateAssertion` is **not** permanent; once the Window ends, a new evaluation is needed unless a **maintenance guard** keeps it valid (see 8.3).
* **RSG‚ÄëC5 (Uniqueness not required).** Multiple states may be asserted for the same Window if their criteria do not conflict (e.g., `Ready` and `Authorized`). **Enactability** is governed by ¬ß8.4.

#### A.2.5:8.3 - Transitions & guards (admission, maintenance, exit)

RSG transitions **express how eligibility changes** when guards fire. Guards are **predicates**; the RSG stays **notation‚Äëneutral**.

* **Admission guard (`‚Üí s`)** declares **conditions to enter** state `s`.
* **Maintenance guard (`s ‚Ü∫`)** must hold to **remain** in `s` (e.g., *FatigueIndex < 0.8*, checked every shift).
* **Exit guard (`s ‚Üí`)** declares **conditions to leave** `s` (e.g., *CalibrationAge > 30d*).

**Rules (RSG‚ÄëG1‚Ä¶G3).**

* **RSG‚ÄëG1 (Checklists vs guards).** Checklists decide **recognition** (‚Äúam I in `s` now?‚Äù). Guards describe **change** (‚Äúwhat moves me in/out of `s`?‚Äù). They may reuse the **same predicates**; their roles are distinct.
* **RSG‚ÄëG2 (No control‚Äëflow).** Guards may refer to **events** (e.g., ‚ÄúCalibration completed‚Äù), but RSG is **not a task graph**; it does not prescribe task order.
* **RSG‚ÄëG3 (Observable basis).** Every guard references **observable** RCS characteristics or recorded events (no hidden timers).

#### A.2.5:8.4 - The **Green‚ÄëGate Law** (enactment gating)

> **Law (RSG‚ÄëE1).** A `U.MethodDescription` step that **requires** role `R` **may be enacted** at Window `W` **iff** there exists a `StateAssertion(holder, R, Context, s, W)` with `s ‚àà S_en`.

Corollaries:

* **RSG‚ÄëE2 (Specialization lift).** If the step requires a **general role** `R`, and the holder has a `StateAssertion` for a **specialist role** `R' ‚â§ R` in an **enactable** state whose **lift** (see ¬ß9.1) is enactable for `R`, the gate passes.
* **RSG‚ÄëE3 (Bundle gate).** If the step requires a **bundle** `R* = R‚ÇÅ ‚äó ‚Ä¶ ‚äó R‚Çô`, enactment requires **n distinct `StateAssertions`** meeting RSG‚ÄëE1 for each `R·µ¢` (unless the Context defines a **CompositeRole** with its own RSG; see ¬ß9.3).
* **RSG‚ÄëE4 (Status‚Äëonly roles).** Roles with `S_en = ‚àÖ` can **never** authorize enactment; they may **gate decisions** (e.g., *ApprovedSpecRole*) but not `U.Work`.


### A.2.5:9 - Interaction with role algebra (`‚â§`, `‚ä•`, `‚äó`) and refinement

#### A.2.5:9.1 - Specialization (`‚â§`) ‚Äî RSG refinement map

When **`R' ‚â§ R`** (Specialist role refines General role) **in the same Context**, their RSGs **must align** by a **refinement map**.

> **Rule (RSG‚ÄëR1 Refinement).** There exists a **surjective mapping**
> `œÄ : S(R') ‚Üí S(R)` such that:
>
> 1. **Enactability preservation:** `s' ‚àà S_en(R') ‚áí œÄ(s') ‚àà S_en(R)`.
> 2. **Checklist entailment:** `Checklist_R'(s') ‚áí Checklist_R(œÄ(s'))` (each specialist state‚Äôs criteria **imply** the general state‚Äôs criteria).
> 3. **Guard monotonicity (informal):** Transitions in `R'` **do not weaken** the general readiness implied by `R` (entering/exiting patterns respect œÄ).

**Interpretation.** Being in `s'` for `R'` *guarantees* being in `œÄ(s')` for `R`. Thus **StateAssertions lift** along œÄ, enabling **RSG‚ÄëE2**.

**Design note.** RCS for `R'` may **extend** that of `R`; specialist states can be **stricter** (more criteria) but not **looser** than their general counterparts.

#### A.2.5:9.2 - Incompatibility (`‚ä•`) ‚Äî state‚Äëaware SoD

`R_A ‚ä• R_B` (within the same Context) states that **a single holder** **must not** have **overlapping, enactable authority** for both roles.

> **Rule (RSG‚ÄëI1).** At **Window `W`**, a holder **violates** `R_A ‚ä• R_B` iff there exist **StateAssertions**
> `‚Ä¶ in s_A ‚àà S_en(R_A)` **and** `‚Ä¶ in s_B ‚àà S_en(R_B)` **both valid at `W`**.

**Optional refinement (soft ‚ä•).** Contexts **may** tighten incompatibility by listing **state pairs** that are forbidden (e.g., `Ready_A ‚ä• Authorized_B`), while allowing benign combinations (e.g., `Suspended_A` + `Ready_B`). By default, **any** enactable pair conflicts.

**Didactic payoff.** SoD is checked by **states in Windows**, not by static role labels.

#### A.2.5:9.3 - Bundles (`‚äó`) ‚Äî conjunction without product explosion

A **bundle role** `R* := R‚ÇÅ ‚äó ‚Ä¶ ‚äó R‚Çô` expresses ‚Äú**must wear all these badges at once**‚Äù.

> **Rule (RSG‚ÄëB1).** If `R*` exists **only as a requirement macro**, **do not** construct a product RSG. The **gate** for a step requiring `R*` is satisfied by **n separate StateAssertions** `s·µ¢ ‚àà S_en(R·µ¢)` at the same Window.

> **Rule (RSG‚ÄëB2 CompositeRole).** If the Context **declares `R*` as a first‚Äëclass `U.Role`**, it **MUST** also specify an `RSG(R*)` and an embedding `Œπ·µ¢ : S(R*) ‚Üí S(R·µ¢)` that **preserves enactability**; being in an enactable state of `R*` **implies** being enactable in each `R·µ¢`.

**Rationale.** Avoid combinatorial blow‚Äëup by default; allow a composite role **only** when the organization genuinely maintains its **own** readiness graph.

#### A.2.5:9.4 - Readiness monotonicity across specialization & bundles

* **RSG‚ÄëM1 (Specialist suffices).** If a step requires `R`, any `R' ‚â§ R` whose **lifted state** is enactable **suffices**.
* **RSG‚ÄëM2 (Bundle conjunctivity).** If a step requires `R‚ÇÅ ‚äó R‚ÇÇ`, the performer must produce **both** gates (two StateAssertions), unless a CompositeRole with RSG exists and is used.


### A.2.5:10 - Guard design (types and discipline)

To keep RSGs **operational** but **not procedural**, guards draw on **observable** inputs only.

**Guard types (non‚Äëexhaustive).**

1. **Threshold guards** over RCS characteristics
   `FatigueIndex < 0.8`, `CalibrationAge ‚â§ 30d`, `EvidenceFreshness(role=Tester) ‚â§ 90d`.
2. **Event guards** (occurrence since last Window)
   `exists SpeechAct(type=Authorization)`, `exists Evaluation(verdict=Pass, checklist=SafetyKit)`.
3. **Temporal guards** (time within range)
   `now ‚àà AuthorizationValidityWindow`, `MaintenanceWindow not active`.
4. **Relational guards**
   `IndependenceFrom(holder=X) = true` (for SoD), `NoOpenIncident(severity‚â•High)`.

**Rules (RSG‚ÄëG4‚Ä¶G6).**

* **RSG‚ÄëG4 (Observable only).** Each guard **MUST** be checkable from **observable artefacts** (observations, work logs, speech acts, evaluations) or present RCS values.
* **RSG‚ÄëG5 (Context‚Äëlocal semantics).** Guard semantics are **scoped to Context**; Cross‚Äëcontext reuse requires a Bridge (¬ß14 in Part 1/4, F.9).
* **RSG‚ÄëG6 (Didactic sparseness).** Prefer **few, stable guards** over many brittle micro‚Äëconditions. If a guard encodes **task order**, you are drifting into workflow; refactor back to eligibility.

Allowed guard evidences include:
* Observation facts (measurements/metrics),
* Evaluation verdicts (checklist results),
* SpeechAct occurrences (communicative `U.Work`), identified by role, act kind, and window (e.g., ‚ÄúApproved(change=4711)‚Äù).

A SpeechAct can change the state (e.g., Prepared‚ÜíAuthorized) but does not by itself satisfy operational steps; it only opens their Green‚ÄëGate.

### A.2.5:11 - Putting it together (one‚Äëscreen mental model)

At any **Window**:

1. **RoleAssignment exists** (A.2.1): `Holder#Role:Context`.
2. **StateAssertion(s) exist**: the holder is **in** one or more **states** as proven by checklists (`U.Evaluation`).
3. **Green‚ÄëGate Law** applies: if at least one asserted state is **enactable**, role‚Äëgated **Method steps** may be enacted; if all are **status‚Äëonly**, the role can **gate decisions** but **not** perform work.
4. **Role algebra** checks: specialization lifts readiness; bundles require **conjunction**; incompatibilities are detected when **two enactable states** coincide for the same holder at the same Window.

This yields a **clean separation**:

* **assignment** (RoleAssignment)
* **Readiness** (RSG + Checklists + StateAssertions)
* **Action** (`U.Work`, gated by RSG)

‚Ä¶and keeps meaning **local**, evidence **observable**, and reasoning **testable**.

### A.2.5:12 - Archetypal RoleStateGraphs (cross‚Äëdomain patterns)

Below are **didactic, reusable** RSG skeletons for the three principal **behavioural** role families and for **epistemic/status** roles. Names and criteria are **context‚Äëlocal**; treat them as **templates** to specialise inside your `U.BoundedContext` (E.10.D1). For each RSG we list:

* **`S`** ‚Äî candidate **States** (enactable states marked **\[E]**);
* **Checklist gist** ‚Äî the **recognition** criteria (cf. ¬ß8.1);
* **Guards** ‚Äî illustrative **admission/maintenance/exit** predicates (cf. ¬ß8.3).

> **Reminder.** Only **enactable** states (**`S_en`**) can open the **Green‚ÄëGate** for `U.Work` (RSG‚ÄëE1). Status‚Äëonly states **gate decisions** but never execution.

#### A.2.5:12.1 - AgentialRole (decision‚Äëcapable actor)

**Context sketch:** `Ops_ChangeManagement_2025`.
**RCS (characteristics, examples):** *CompetenceLevel, FatigueIndex, IndependenceFlag, AuthorizationValidity, IncidentLoad, RiskClass.*

**States `S`**

* **Unprepared** ‚Äî training incomplete; checklists fail.
* **Prepared** ‚Äî training + competence thresholds met.
* **Authorized** ‚Äî valid approval window present. **\[E]**
* **Ready** ‚Äî `Prepared ‚àß Authorized ‚àß FatigueIndex < œÑ`. **\[E]**
* **Active** ‚Äî contemporaneous **`U.Work`** step is underway under this role (**with a valid StateAssertion in the window**). **\[E]**
* **Suspended** ‚Äî temporary block (incident/conflict).
* **Revoked** ‚Äî authorization expired/withdrawn.

**Checklist gist**

* *Prepared*: certificates valid; recency of practice ‚â§ X; simulator score ‚â• Y.
* *Authorized*: `exists SpeechAct(type=Approval, scope=Role, age‚â§30d)`.
* *Ready*: *Prepared ‚àß Authorized ‚àß* independence from conflicting work; fatigue within limits.

**Guards**

* Admission `‚Üí Prepared`: `ExamPassed ‚àß SimulatorScore‚â•Y`.
* Admission `‚Üí Authorized`: presence of approval speech‚Äëact within window.
* Maintenance `Ready ‚Ü∫`: `FatigueIndex<œÑ ‚àß IncidentLoad‚â§k`.
* Exit `Ready ‚Üí Suspended`: high‚Äëseverity incident assigned OR SoD violation detected.
* Exit `Authorized ‚Üí Revoked`: window elapsed or explicit revoke speech‚Äëact.

#### A.2.5:12.2 - TransformerRole (non‚Äëagential executor of change)

**Context sketch:** `PlantOps_Pipeline_2025`.
**RCS:** *CalibrationAge, SafetyInterlock, SelfTestPass, EnvRangeOK, DegradationIndex.*

**States `S`**

* **Unavailable** ‚Äî offline, missing prerequisites.
* **Calibrated** ‚Äî calibration fresh; self‚Äëtest ok.
* **Permitted** ‚Äî safety interlocks clear; clearance token valid.
* **Ready** ‚Äî `Calibrated ‚àß Permitted ‚àß EnvRangeOK`. **\[E]**
* **Running** ‚Äî executing a method step (**with contemporaneous StateAssertion**). **\[E]**
* **Degraded** ‚Äî still operable under derated envelope. **\[E]** (if policy allows)
* **Quarantined** ‚Äî suspected hazard; no enactment.

**Checklist gist**

* *Calibrated*: `CalibrationAge‚â§30d ‚àß SelfTestPass=true`.
* *Permitted*: `SafetyInterlock = Clear ‚àß NoOpenIncident(sev‚â•High)`.
* *Ready*: *Calibrated ‚àß Permitted ‚àß* environment in spec.

**Guards**

* Admission `‚Üí Calibrated`: calibration record timestamp ‚â§30d.
* Maintenance `Ready ‚Ü∫`: env sensors within limits; no new hazard event.
* Exit `Ready ‚Üí Quarantined`: detected leak OR hazard alarm.
* Transition `Running ‚Üí Ready`: step completed ‚àß cool‚Äëdown satisfied.
* Transition `Ready ‚Üí Degraded`: `DegradationIndex‚àà[d‚ÇÅ,d‚ÇÇ]` ‚àß derate policy active.

#### A.2.5:12.3 - ObserverRole (measurement actor, incl. SOSA/SSN style)

**Context sketch:** `Lab_Thermo_2025`.
**RCS:** *CalibrationAge, TraceabilityChainOK, DriftRate, SyncError, CleanlinessScore.*

**States `S`**

* **Unqualified** ‚Äî no metrological chain.
* **Calibrated** ‚Äî with traceability to standard.
* **Synchronized** ‚Äî time/phase sync within tolerance.
* **In‚ÄëRange** ‚Äî drift & environment within spec.
* **Measuring** ‚Äî performing observation. **\[E]**
* **Stale** ‚Äî calibration or sync expired.
* **Quarantined** ‚Äî suspect bias/contamination.

**Checklist gist**

* *Calibrated*: traceability cert valid; calibration within period.
* *Synchronized*: `SyncError‚â§Œµ`.
* *In‚ÄëRange*: drift ‚â§ threshold; contamination tests passed.
* *Measuring*: *Calibrated ‚àß Synchronized ‚àß In‚ÄëRange* AND observation procedure active.

**Guards**

* Admission `‚Üí Calibrated`: calibration event recorded < 180d.
* Exit `Calibrated ‚Üí Stale`: calibration age > threshold.
* Exit `In‚ÄëRange ‚Üí Quarantined`: contamination alert OR failed control sample.
* Transition `Measuring ‚Üí In‚ÄëRange`: procedure complete.

> **Note.** Many ObserverRole states are **pre‚Äëenactment** gates; only **Measuring** is enactable.


#### A.2.5:12.4 - Epistemic/status roles (no enactment)

These roles are **status‚Äëonly**; **`S_en = ‚àÖ`**. They **gate decisions** (e.g., can be cited, can constrain), but can never authorize `U.Work`.

##### A.2.5:12.4.1 - NormativeStandardRole

**States:** *Draft*, *Candidate*, *Approved*, *Superseded*, *Deprecated*.
**Checklist gist:** governance decision records; publication identifiers; supersession links.
**Guards:** *Approved ‚Üí Superseded* on adoption of newer edition; *Candidate ‚Üí Approved* after ratification vote.

##### A.2.5:12.4.2 - EvidenceRole

**States:** *Collected*, *Verified*, *Validated*, *Obsolete*, *Contested*.
**Checklist gist:** verification/validation `U.Evaluation` present; freshness window; reproducibility tag.
**Guards:** decay to *Obsolete* by age; transition to *Contested* upon counter‚Äëevidence.

##### A.2.5:12.4.3 - RequirementRole

**States:** *Proposed*, *Accepted*, *Implemented*, *Verified*, *Waived*.
**Checklist gist:** acceptance decision; trace links to `U.Work`; verification report; waiver authorization.
**Guards:** *Accepted ‚Üí Implemented* when linked executions close; *Implemented ‚Üí Verified* on passed acceptance checklist; *Any ‚Üí Waived* by authorized speech‚Äëact.
