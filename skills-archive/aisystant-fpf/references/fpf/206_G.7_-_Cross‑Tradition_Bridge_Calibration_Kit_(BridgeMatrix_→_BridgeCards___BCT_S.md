## G.7 - Cross‚ÄĎTradition Bridge Calibration Kit (BridgeMatrix ‚Üí BridgeCards + BCT/Sentinels)

**Tag.** Architectural pattern
**Stage.** design‚ÄĎtime (calibration + publication) + run‚ÄĎtime (sentinel‚ÄĎdriven telemetry emission; orchestration owned by **G.11**)
**Primary output.** A bridge calibration kit that turns **G.2**‚Äôs BridgeMatrix rows into **F.9** `BridgeCard`s and publishes: a `BridgeCalibrationTable (BCT)` + `CalibrationLedger` + `RegressionSet` + `SentinelSet`, plus UTS‚ÄĎvisible crossing rows and RSCR‚ÄĎready sentinel triggers scoped to `PathSliceId` / `PatternScopeId`.
**Primary hooks.** `G.Core` (Part‚ÄĎG invariants + RSCR trigger catalogue + default-ownership index), **G.2** (BridgeMatrix), **F.9** (BridgeCard + CL/CL^k), **F.3/F.7** (SenseCell anchoring; row bottleneck discipline), **E.18/A.21** (GateCrossing + CrossingBundle checks), **G.6** (PathId/PathSliceId citation surface), **G.5** (downstream consumer for eligibility/selection), **G.11** (refresh orchestration consumer), **B.3** (assurance lanes + penalty policies), **C.21** (DHC accounts such as AlignmentDensity), **C.18/C.19** (QD/OEE pins when relevant), **C.23** (SoS‚ÄĎLOG clauses as explainability gates for cross‚ÄĎTradition choices), **G.4** (Acceptance hooks/thresholds when bridges are used as selector gates), **E.10** (LEX / strict distinction discipline).
**Working‚ÄĎModel first.** Prefer a minimal, auditable calibration procedure and worked micro‚ÄĎcases; escalate to heavier harnesses only where risk warrants (per **E.8**).
**Non‚ÄĎduplication note.** Universal Part‚ÄĎG invariants (no shadow specs; Bridge‚ÄĎonly crossings; penalty routing to `R_eff` only; P2W split; typed/id‚ÄĎbased RSCR causes; single‚ÄĎowner defaults; őĒ‚ÄĎdiscipline) are owned by `G.Core` and are *cited* via `CC‚ÄĎGCORE‚ÄĎ*`. This pattern defines only the *bridge calibration kit* and its surfaces.

### G.7:1 - Problem frame

SoTA synthesis (**G.2**) can legitimately preserve pluralism by exporting a **BridgeMatrix**: a Tradition√óTradition inventory of ‚Äúcomparable constructs‚ÄĚ with preliminary notes (candidate correspondences, likely losses, tentative levels). Downstream patterns (CHR/CAL/selector/logging/shipping) cannot consume this safely unless cross‚ÄĎContext reuse is:

* **materialised** as explicit bridge artefacts (not implied by prose),
* **calibrated** with a small, auditable procedure (so CL/CL^k/plane routing is not a narrative),
* **published** as checkable crossing bundles (UTS + GateCrossing harness),
* **refreshable** in a *targeted* way (path‚ÄĎscoped RSCR rather than whole‚ÄĎpack reruns).

`G.7` packages this into a kit: `BCT` + `BridgeCard` publication + `RegressionSet`/`SentinelSet` wiring, so that later patterns can satisfy core invariants without re‚ÄĎinventing cross‚ÄĎTradition machinery.

### G.7:2 - Problem

1. Cross‚ÄĎTradition comparisons are frequently attempted via informal ‚Äúsynonymy‚ÄĚ or ad‚ÄĎhoc mappings, causing silent meaning drift and hidden crossings.
2. Plane mismatches (world ‚ÜĒ concept ‚ÜĒ episteme, or other `ReferencePlane` shifts) are often ignored, or conflated with ‚Äúsemantic sameness‚ÄĚ, causing wrong downstream confidence.
3. Calibration changes (CL/CL^k/plane or their policy pins) must trigger **targeted** re‚ÄĎchecks; pack‚ÄĎwide reweaves are too costly and too slow.
4. If bridges are involved in QD/illumination or other edition‚ÄĎsensitive telemetry, **edition pins** must be tracked (otherwise comparisons become irreproducible after a map/distance/policy update).
5. Row‚ÄĎlevel summaries (for matrix rows / comparable construct groups) tend to be averaged or ‚Äúsmoothed‚ÄĚ, which is incompatible with bottleneck semantics and loss honesty.

### G.7:3 - Forces

| Force                                    | Tension                                                                                                                                                                   |
| ---------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Comparability vs local authority**     | Enable comparisons across Traditions ‚ÜĒ avoid overriding Context‚ÄĎlocal meaning.                                                                                            |
| **Auditability vs authoring throughput** | Require explicit artefacts, losses, and pins ‚ÜĒ keep the calibration procedure light enough to be used.                                                                    |
| **Targeted refresh vs safety**           | Emit path‚ÄĎlocal RSCR triggers ‚ÜĒ ensure triggers are typed and carry enough payload pins for audit and rerun planning.                                                     |
| **Plane awareness vs ‚Äúone story‚ÄĚ**       | Explicitly surface `ReferencePlane` and plane penalties ‚ÜĒ avoid turning plane discussion into a second semantics of ‚Äúsameness‚ÄĚ.                                           |
| **QD comparability vs metric drift**     | Enable cross‚ÄĎcontext reporting of archive/illumination telemetry ‚ÜĒ enforce edition‚ÄĎaware pins for descriptor/distance/policies only when those modes are actually in use. |

##### G.7:4.2.1 - CL / CL^k admissibility regime and plane guard (kit‚ÄĎlocal; normative)

This subsection is kit‚ÄĎowned (G.7) and complements (but does not duplicate) `G.Core` penalty routing and tri‚ÄĎstate guard semantics.

**Admissibility regimes (row‚ÄĎlevel, minimal).**
* `RowCL_min` MUST take a value in `{3,2,1,0}` (value set and CL meaning are owned by F.9; G.7 owns the admissibility regime).
* Default admissibility for cross‚ÄĎTradition reuse:
  * `RowCL_min ‚Č• 2` ‚áí admissible for reuse (subject to downstream guards/policies).
  * `RowCL_min = 1` ‚áí **NOT** admissible unless an explicit `WaiverRef[]` is cited; any reuse under waiver is **guarded-only** (no substitution semantics).
  * `RowCL_min = 0` ‚áí forbidden for reuse; it MAY remain in BCT as a documented non‚ÄĎbridge with loss notes/counterexamples.
* **Honesty rule (row‚ÄĎlevel):**
* if `RowCL_min ‚Č§ 2`, at least one `CounterExampleRef[]` MUST be cited;
* if `RowCL_min = 3` and `CounterExampleRef[]` is empty, a citable `CounterExampleAbsenceRef` MUST be provided (explicit ‚Äúsearched‚ÄĎnone found / no known counterexample‚ÄĚ disclosure);
  * if any `LossNoteRef[]` is present, the row MUST NOT be presented as ‚Äúfree substitution‚ÄĚ in any consumer surface.

**Kind channel (`CL^k`) (conditional).**
If a row relies on bridges in the `Kind` channel, then `RowCL_k_min` and `ő®(CL^k)` pin MUST be present, and the same admissibility regimes apply to `RowCL_k_min`.

**Plane guard (`CL^plane`) (conditional).**
If `ReferencePlane(src)` and `ReferencePlane(tgt)` differ (or plane routing is explicitly invoked), then:
* `RowCL_plane_min` and `ő¶_plane` pin MUST be present;
* if either plane pin is absent, the row is non‚ÄĎconformant (no implicit plane defaulting);
* any ‚Äúblocking‚ÄĚ outcome must be representable downstream via `G.Core` tri‚ÄĎstate guard (`abstain` or a policy‚ÄĎbound `degrade(mode=‚Ä¶)`), without introducing additional statuses in G.7;

#### G.7:4.3 - Minimal calibration procedure (auditable; table‚ÄĎbacked; bridge‚ÄĎfirst)

For each Tradition‚ÄĎpair and each comparable construct row from **G.2**:

1. **Materialise bridge artefacts.** Produce (or reuse) **F.9** `BridgeCard`s for the concrete `SenseCell`‚ÄĎlevel alignments required by the row scope.
   *Note.* ‚ÄúSenseCell anchoring‚ÄĚ is a kit requirement: if a row is authored at a coarser token level, the SenseCell anchors must be explicitly cited (F.3 discipline).
2. **Record row scope and losses.** Author a `RowScopeId` and record loss notes as first‚ÄĎclass citations (e.g., `LossNoteRef[]`), not as informal footnotes.
   Also record `RowCL_min` (and `RowCL_k_min?`, `RowCL_plane_min?` when applicable) and cite `WaiverRef[]` if any row is intentionally kept at `=1` for guarded-only reuse.
3. **Plane pins (no hidden plane mixing).** Record `ReferencePlane` pins for source/target and the relevant policy id pins for plane routing (ids only; do not duplicate policy tables).
4. **Policy pins for penalty routing.** Record the policy id pins needed to audit penalty routing (ids only). Penalty semantics are core‚ÄĎowned (route via `CC‚ÄĎGCORE‚ÄĎPEN‚ÄĎ1`); G.7‚Äôs responsibility is to make the pins explicit and published.
5. **Row bottleneck discipline.** When a row aggregates multiple bridge cells, row summarisation uses bottleneck semantics (F.7) and carries a counterexample citation whenever any cell is loss‚ÄĎnoted.
6. **Regression and sentinel wiring.** Create/update the `RegressionSet` and `SentinelSet`. Any calibration change that can affect downstream audit (CL/CL^k/plane pins, relevant policy ids, edition pins for involved telemetry surfaces, freshness window) emits typed RSCR triggers (canonical ids; scope + payload pins).
   If the regression harness is run, record a citable `RegressionRunRef` (or equivalent run/delta reference) and attach it to the relevant ledger entries (pin‚ÄĎfirst; no narrative-only deltas).

### G.7:5 - Archetypal Grounding (System / Episteme)

**System (őď_sys):** *Cross‚ÄĎstandard safety assurance comparison (bridge‚ÄĎfirst).*
A team must compare a safety assurance claim across two regulatory Traditions (e.g., a ‚Äúfunctional safety case‚ÄĚ tradition and a ‚ÄúML system testing‚ÄĚ tradition) for the *same physical system scope*. `G.7` forces explicit SenseCell‚ÄĎlevel bridges (what exactly is the ‚Äúhazard‚ÄĚ, what is the ‚Äúevidence carrier‚ÄĚ, what is the ‚Äúpass criterion‚ÄĚ), records losses, pins planes, and provides sentinels so that changes in the safety evidence protocol editions trigger path‚ÄĎlocal RSCR rather than re‚ÄĎauthoring the entire safety case.

**Episteme (őď_epist):** *Benchmark protocol pluralism (post‚ÄĎ2015 evaluation practice).*
A research group wants to compare ‚Äústate‚ÄĎof‚ÄĎthe‚ÄĎart‚ÄĚ across multiple evaluation Traditions (IID performance, shift robustness, preference‚ÄĎbased evaluation). `G.7` turns ‚Äúthese are comparable‚ÄĚ into explicit BridgeCards with declared row scope, pins the evaluation protocol editions, and emits sentinels so that when a benchmark protocol or policy pin changes, downstream selector decisions can be re‚ÄĎaudited by re‚ÄĎciting the same PathSlice‚ÄĎscoped evidence.

### G.7:8 - Common Anti-Patterns and How to Avoid Them

* **Bridge‚ÄĎby‚ÄĎprose (‚Äúthey mean the same thing‚ÄĚ).**
  **Avoid:** publish BCT rows + BridgeCards + UTS rows; require SenseCell anchoring and row scopes.
* **SenseFamily jump (scope‚ÄĎbridge used as kind‚ÄĎbridge).**
  **Avoid:** keep channel/sense‚ÄĎfamily constraints owned by **F.9** visible; use `RowScopeId` to state which channel is claimed, and require `CL^k` + `ő®(CL^k)` pins when a kind‚ÄĎchannel bridge is invoked (do not ‚Äúupgrade‚ÄĚ a scope‚ÄĎchannel bridge into kind substitution).
* **Plane blindness (‚Äúconcept = world‚ÄĚ).**
  **Avoid:** record plane pins and policy id pins; keep plane effects auditable and separable from CL/CL^k semantics.
* **CL smoothing / averaging.**
  **Avoid:** enforce row bottleneck summaries and counterexample citations for loss‚ÄĎnoted cells.
* **Pack‚ÄĎwide refresh on a local bridge edit.**
  **Avoid:** register sentinels scoped to `PathSliceId` and emit typed RSCR triggers with minimal payload pins.
* **QD metric drift by unpinned artefacts.**
  **Avoid:** enable `G.7:Ext.QDParityPins` only when needed and require edition/policy pins when enabled.

* **Why a kit (not a new governance card or legality gate)?** Bridge calibration must support many downstream consumers without becoming a competing legality gate; contract semantics remain owned by `CG‚ÄĎSpec`/`CN‚ÄĎSpec`.
